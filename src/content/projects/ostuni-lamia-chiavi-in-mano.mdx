## About Chiavi in Mano Villas in Ostuni

Chiavi in mano contracts consolidate land plot, architectural design, construction, MEP installation, and interior finishes under one promoter or coordinator. Foreign buyers benefit because milestone payments align with construction progress rather than separate invoices to masons, electricians, and pool installers.

Pregio Immobiliare markets this lamia scheme within Ostuni's white-city tourism orbit. Ostuni draws summer visitors from Brindisi airport (roughly 45 minutes) and Bari (about 90 minutes), supporting furnished rental demand for well-finished two-bedroom countryside villas.

The lamia form signals regional authenticity. Modern lamia builds add double glazing, solar-ready roofs, and compliant septic or municipal sewage connections required for habitability certificates at rogito.

## Investment Pros and Cons

### Advantages

- Turnkey structure reduces foreign-owner construction risk
- Lamia aesthetic supports premium STR marketing versus generic box villas
- Ostuni tourism and expat demand for countryside stays
- Smaller 95 sqm footprint lowers IMU and maintenance versus compound villas
- Puglia price point often below Tuscany comparable stock

### Risks

- POA pricing obscures yield math until quote received
- Agricultural land conversion and building permits remain promoter responsibility
- Rural utilities (water, sewage) need verification on specific plot
- STR requires CIN registration and regional compliance
- Off-plan delay exposure until habitability certificate

## Rental Market Outlook

Ostuni countryside villas with pool and lamia character often achieve €1,200-2,500 weekly in peak summer on compliant short-term platforms. Long-term winter leases to remote workers run €800-1,200 monthly for two-bedroom furnished units, lower than Milan but with lower entry ticket.

Gross yields on €400k-550k turnkey stock often land near 4-6% blended if STR season is managed professionally. Net yields fall after management, cleaning, and cedolare secca.

## Closing verification checklist

Before compromesso on Ostuni lamia chiavi in mano stock, verify plot class, promoter scope, and rural utility compliance in writing.

**Chiavi in mano scope.** Confirm whether POA bundle includes pool, boundary walls, gates, landscaping, appliances, and septic or municipal sewage connections. Lamia projects often omit pool or landscaping from base quotes.

**Land and permits.** Agricultural contrada plots require conversion clarity. Request building permit, SCIA or equivalent, and geometric survey on the exact cadastral parcel before reservation deposit.

**Milestone schedule.** Chiavi in mano contracts should tie payments to certified construction stages: foundation, envelope, MEP, finishes, habitability. Delay penalties must reference realistic 12-18 month rural build timelines.

**STR and CIN path.** If underwriting STR, verify comune registration requirements for countryside villas before marketing. Puglia municipalities tightened CIN enforcement on pool villas in 2024-2026 cycles.

**Tax and IMU.** Model IMU on rural property class and registration tax at 2% or 9% depending on residency election. Budget 8-12% total buyer costs including notary on Puglia countryside purchases.
