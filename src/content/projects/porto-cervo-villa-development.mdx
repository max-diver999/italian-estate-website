## Consorzio and Marina Compliance

Consorzio Costa Smeralda architectural palette restricts visible materials to approved stone, timber, and colour ranges on new villas and extensions. Non-compliant renovations face enforcement orders and resale discounts when buyers discover unapproved pool fencing or rooftop additions during due diligence.

Porto Cervo marina walkability commands premiums over hillside villas requiring car descent to evening dining and yacht tender pickup. Pevero Golf frontage adds second lifestyle anchor for buyers wanting fairway views alongside sea glimpses.

Ultra-luxury staffing models on Costa Smeralda villas often include estate manager, pool technician, and seasonal chef contracts. Budget €80,000-150,000 annual operating costs on staffed €3,000,000+ estates before IMU and concession fees.

New-build infill plots near Romazzino and Capriccioli pass lengthy consorzio design reviews before municipal SCIA approval. Buyers acquiring raw land with approved plans should verify timeline to habitability and builder track record on prior Gallura deliveries.

Yachting regatta weeks in July and August concentrate marina social demand and villa weekly rental peaks exceeding €25,000-40,000 on staffed compounds with dock access. Shoulder season occupancy requires proactive marketing to Nordic and German families seeking quiet September sailing before school terms resume.

Maritime demaniale concessions on beach structures expire independently from land title. Renewal negotiations with port authority can impose capital upgrades on jetties and sea terraces that sellers defer until buyer discovery phase.
