## About Sanremo Apartment Stock

Sanremo combines classic Riviera harbour imagery, mild winter climate marketing, and apartment inventory depth that Portofino cannot match at comparable per sqm. Tranio and Green-acres seed portfolios list 1960s-1990s residential towers and palazzi with terraces oriented toward the Ligurian Sea and harbour fireworks festival calendars.

French cross-border buyers from Nice metropolitan area and Milan professionals seeking weekend coast bases dominate international enquiry. German retirees occasionally target furnished long-term leases on sea-view units with elevator access and underground parking inclusion.

Each listing represents independent seller stock. Buyers should verify terrace conformità, elevator maintenance records, and sea-view obstruction risk from future construction on corso hillside lanes before compromesso signatures.

Model gross yield using 60-75% annual occupancy on well-managed sea-view units rather than peak-only marketing claims.

## Investment Analysis

Gross yields on €450,000-650,000 sea-view two-bedroom units often land in 3-5% range when licensed and professionally managed with terrace photography and parking highlighted in listings. Net yields compress after IMU, management at 18-25%, and cedolare secca on furnished short stays.

Capital appreciation tracked cross-border French buyer enquiry when euro pricing dipped against franc purchasing power cycles. Resale liquidity improves on elevator-served buildings with deeded parking because Milan buyers reject walk-up stock above second floor without lift service.

Renovation of 1970s apartments runs €80,000-150,000 for MEP, bathrooms, and terrace waterproofing when sellers market partial restoration status without conformità clearance on enclosed balconies common in Ligurian tower stock.

Transaction stack on €500,000 second-home purchase runs 10-12% including registration tax, notary, and agency fees. STR launch adds €10,000-18,000 for compliant furniture and CIN setup before first booking season.

Aurelia summer traffic can delay Nice airport transfers during festival weekends. STR operators should document private transfer partners and early check-in logistics in guest communications to protect review scores.

## Cross-Border Buyer and Tax Notes

French buyers from Nice and Menton often compare Sanremo per sqm against Côte d'Azur stock where parking and elevator service are scarcer at comparable sea-view tickets. Italian registration tax at second-home rates applies regardless of EU citizenship, so cross-border purchasers should model 9-11% transaction stack before furnishing budgets rather than copying French agency fee assumptions.

Milan weekend owners frequently register STR activity with commercialista guidance on cedolare secca versus ordinary rental regimes when personal use exceeds 90 nights annually. Liguria CIR display rules require portal compliance updates after 2024 national STR reforms, and professional operators should confirm commune registration before marketing multi-unit portfolios.

Winter heating and dehumidification costs on sea-facing terraces run €2,000-4,500 annually on 80-100 sqm units when owners want year-round remote-worker occupancy experiments instead of pure summer tourism calendars.

Elevator maintenance reserves on 1970s towers should be reviewed in condominium financial statements because special assessments for lift replacement can exceed €15,000 per unit and delay resale if buyers discover deferred capex after compromesso.
