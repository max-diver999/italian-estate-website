## About the Olympic Village Conversion

COIMA delivered the Olympic Village ahead of original 2025 schedules for Milan Cortina 2026, then hosted athletes during the Winter Games window. Fondazione Milano Cortina 2026 handed facilities back for conversion into student housing with furnishings already aligned to final student layout, reducing waste versus dual furniture plans.

COIMA press releases describe a roughly four-month conversion from Games mode to student residence, among the fastest post-event conversions cited for major global Games villages. Target opening August 2026 supports September 2026 academic year occupancy.

The village sits within Porta Romana railway yard urban regeneration, linking Metro Line M3 access, university districts, and southern Milan employment nodes.

## Student Housing Model

Unlike [Inspire UpTown Milan](/projects/inspire-uptown-milan/) condo sales, Olympic Village inventory is bed allocations:

| Allocation type | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| University blocks | Bocconi and public university agreements |
| Subsidized beds | MUR-compliant reduced rates including approx. €250/month tiers on subset |
| Direct applications | villaggio.coima.com portal |
| DSU beds | Right-to-study allocations with public universities |

Foreign property investors cannot typically purchase individual beds as rogito real estate. Investment exposure is indirect through nearby rental demand and land value spillover.

## Location and Spillover Investment Thesis

Porta Romana connects southern Milan to centro via Metro M3. Student housing at Olympic Village increases demand for:

- Furnished apartments within 15 minutes of Metro M3 for students not allocated village beds
- Restaurants, gyms, and services catering to international students
- Long-term leases from university staff and young professionals priced out of village allocations

Landlords buying compliant apartments nearby should underwrite tenant profiles on their specific building, not assume automatic occupancy from village overflow.

## Pros and Cons for Property Investors

### Advantages

Structural student housing demand in Milan meets only about one-third of need per COIMA press citations, supporting long-term rental narratives near Porta Romana. COIMA delivery credibility reduces execution risk perception for district regeneration.

### Disadvantages

Cannot buy inside village as standard foreign-buyer condo. Nearby apartment tickets still reflect Milan pricing with 3.5-4.5% gross yield bands typical for furnished stock, not student-housing subsidized rates.

### Risk Mitigation

Underwrite spillover apartments on their own tenant data. Track villaggio.coima.com occupancy announcements as demand signal, not as substitute for building-level due diligence.

## Metro M3 and Tenant Micro-Markets

Porta Romana Metro M3 stations connect to Duomo, Centrale rail hub, and San Donato corporate corridor within 20-30 minutes peak commute windows students and young professionals accept when saving on subsidized village beds.

Landlords should map walking minutes to M3 honestly; listings claiming Porta Romana proximity from beyond Viale Tibaldi often receive student complaints that hurt review scores and renewal rates.

Furnished micro-units and one-bedroom apartments with elevator access outperform large family units for student spillover when kitchens and desks are photographed clearly in listings.

## Tax and Compliance for Nearby Landlords

Furnished landlords targeting students must register cedolare secca elections before first rent invoice and confirm regolamento allows student sublets in buildings with mixed owner-occupier and rental profiles.

Non-resident owners need commercialista setup for IMU and rental reporting; student tenants may require registered lease contracts for university enrollment documentation, increasing administrative workload versus casual STR guests.

## Bocconi and University Allocation Context

COIMA press releases cite substantial bed blocks allocated to Bocconi University and subsidized beds meeting Ministry of University and Research requirements, including approximately €250 per month tiers on a subset of units for eligible students.

Those subsidized rates apply inside village allocations, not to nearby private landlords who must still price at market furnished rates students accept when village beds are full.
