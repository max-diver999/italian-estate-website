## About Costa Smeralda Apartment Stock

Costa Smeralda apartment inventory fills a gap between inland Arzachena value stock and €1.5 million+ Porto Cervo villa tickets, offering marina walkability and sea glimpses with lower staffing overhead than large villa compounds. Engel & Voelkers Gallura and syndicated portals market 1970s-1990s consorzio-compliant residences with pool access and shared garden rights.

German and Swiss families with multi-decade Gallura ties form deepest resale audience on correctly priced sea-view apartments with documented conformità. Nordic yachting buyers occasionally target furnished summer leases on Capo di Volpe peninsula units near marina social calendars.

Consorzio Costa Smeralda architectural controls apply to exterior colours, roof forms, and terrace additions on renovations. Buyers planning enclosure upgrades should commission geometra pre-checks against approved consorzio palettes before compromesso deposits.

## Consorzio and STR Mechanics

Licensed ultra-luxury STR on sea-view apartments can achieve strong July-August weekly rates with lower maintenance than staffed villa compounds, but gross annual yields still compress on €700,000+ tickets after IMU and management at 20-30%. Many owners use apartments for personal regatta weeks with selective STR when away.

Maritime concessions on shared beach structures and sea-level terraces remain separate licences from apartment deeds. Transferability audits prevent buyers discovering expired concessions after rogito when listings marketed private beach access.

Commune STR rules and condominium minutes on tourist density should be reviewed before underwriting yields copied from hotel occupancy statistics during peak yachting weeks alone.

## Investment Analysis

Gross yields on €650,000-900,000 sea-view two-bedroom apartments often land in 2-4% range when licensed for ultra-luxury summer rentals with professional management. Net yields compress after IMU, concession fees where applicable, and management on trophy band stock.

Capital preservation thesis dominates: consorzio scarcity and marina brand support long-term positioning versus yield-focused buyers who redirect capital to southern Italy or interior Sardinia value markets.

Resale liquidity improves on Porto Rotondo and Romazzino apartments with parking, elevator access, and unobstructed sea glimpses because German family buyers reject walk-up stock without lift service despite lower headline prices.

Transaction stack on €750,000 second-home purchase runs 10-12% including registration tax, notary, and survey fees. Ultra-luxury STR launch adds €15,000-30,000 for designer furniture and concierge onboarding before regatta season marketing.

Summer SS125 traffic can add 30-45 minutes to Olbia airport transfers during peak weekends. Apartment owners marketing STR should document helicopter and private transfer alternatives for ultra-HNWI guest expectations.

## Consorzio Carry and Regatta Calendar

Apartment owners in Porto Rotondo often coordinate marina berth rentals separately from apartment deeds because yachting weeks drive guest expectations for tender pickup logistics within walking distance of Capo di Volpe harbour. Budget €15,000-40,000 annually for berth access when marketing ultra-luxury STR packages that include marina lifestyle rather than apartment-only beach days.

Consorzio fines for unauthorised terrace awnings or exterior paint deviations can delay resale marketing when previous owners ignored palette rules visible from coastal roads. Pre-purchase geometra review should photograph every exterior element and compare against consorzio filing archives, not only agency renderings.

German family buyers dominate spring and autumn inspection windows before regatta season, so sellers who list in November-January often capture motivated HNWI bids before summer traffic compresses negotiation timelines on correctly priced sea-glimpse stock with deeded parking.

Apartment insurance for ultra-luxury furnished STR should include contents coverage for designer kitchens and art installations because standard homeowner policies often exclude high-value tenant damage above €50,000 per incident.
