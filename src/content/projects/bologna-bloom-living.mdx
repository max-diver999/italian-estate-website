## About Bologna Bloom

Bologna Bloom Living reactivates the ex mercato Navile comparto within Bologna's northern Navile district, a post-industrial corridor undergoing supermarket, student housing, and residential phases after years of masterplan delays. Prelios through Fondo Fidia secured building permission for the first lot delivering 145 alloggi with polifunctional resident services and Class A envelopes designed by Lombardini22.

Two eleven-storey towers and a four-storey lower body host monolocali from 50-52 sqm, bilocali from 63-79 sqm, trilocali to 115 sqm, quadrilocali to 157 sqm, and plurilocali to 175 sqm on Abitare Co listings in 2026. Ground floors dedicate space to portineria, bicycle storage, children's areas, fitness zones, and flexible community rooms while two underground levels provide box auto and posti auto.

First Atlantic Real Estate advises the transaction while Giuseppe Crupi at Abitare Co markets units as a human-scale project integrating green courtyards with contemporary Bologna living. Ugo Debernardi highlights Navile as one of Bologna's most significant social and economic regeneration zones reconnecting industrial heritage with services-rich residential product.

Adjacent construction already advances a new supermarket and a student residence for about 400 students, tightening weekday demand for furnished rentals and long-term leases when Bloom residents seek neighborhood completeness without centro historic density premiums.

## Off-Plan Mechanics

Standard Italian off-plan process applies: reservation deposit, compromesso con milestone payments tied to construction progress, bank guarantees on developer deposits, and rogito at handover with notary and commercialista coordinating registration tax election between prima casa and second home treatments before compromesso signature.

November 2027 delivery implies roughly 18-month capital lock-up from mid-2026 enquiry windows without rental income unless buyers negotiate early assignment rights with Abitare Co and Prelios fund counsel. Budget 9-11% transaction costs on €250,000-400,000 typical bilocale tickets including registration tax, notary, and agency fees on any resale assignment.

Class A plant reduces tenant utility volatility for inclusive lease packages marketed to hospital fellows, AV station commuters, and university researchers on 12-24 month contracts. STR strategies face Bologna commune density rules and condominium bylaws on sub-30-day lets in new towers; long-term furnished lease exit paths often deliver stabler net yields.
