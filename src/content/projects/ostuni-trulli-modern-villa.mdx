## Heritage and Historical Significance

The trulli complex dates to the mid-16th century, originally serving as seasonal accommodation for olive harvesters. Historical records indicate the property belonged to the Benedictine monastery of Ostuni, making it part of the region's religious and agricultural heritage.

### Architectural Features
- Four conical roofs with original stone construction
- Chiancarelle limestone roof tiles, hand-placed
- Dry stone walls using ancient building techniques  
- Original floors with traditional Pugliese stone
- Cistern system for water collection, still functional
- Stone oven for bread baking, fully restored

The heritage approval process required extensive archaeological surveys and architectural documentation. Apulia Deluxe worked closely with the Soprintendenza (Italian heritage authority) to ensure all restoration work meets stringent conservation standards.

This approval grants significant advantages: protected heritage status enhances property value and provides tax benefits for restoration costs. Additionally, the permits prevent future development nearby, preserving the property's exclusive rural setting.

### Cultural Context
Trulli construction represents UNESCO-recognized building techniques unique to Puglia. The conical design provides natural climate control, while dry stone construction allows seasonal expansion and contraction. These features make trulli remarkably resilient and comfortable year-round.

The property's olive grove contains 180 productive trees, some estimated over 200 years old. Annual olive oil production averages 800-1,200 liters, providing both personal use and potential commercial revenue. The grove includes heritage varieties specific to the Ostuni terroir.
