## Entry Prices and Affordability

Naples remains one of Italy's most affordable major cities on a per-metre basis among prestige-address candidates.

City-wide averages sit €2,200-3,200/m² in 2026. Vomero and Chiaia hills command €3,500-5,000/m² for sea-view and volcanic-stone palazzi with elevators. Centro storico UNESCO stock ranges €2,800-4,500/m² depending on floor and seismic retrofit status.

Rome's Lazio average near €3,779/m² understates prime pricing. Trastevere, Prati, and Parioli trade €3,500-6,500/m² per our [Rome property investment guide](/guides/rome-property-investment-guide/). Outer EUR and Ostiense offer €2,800-4,200/m² regeneration plays.

Naples delivers more square metres per euro in comparable hilltop prestige. Rome delivers more foreign marketing recognition per euro spent in Trastevere.

## Rental Yields and Income Strategies

Naples is the yield leader of this pairing. Well-bought Vomero long-term units achieve 3.5-4.5% gross. Compliant STR near centro and cruise-port spillover can reach 4.5-6% gross before tax, higher than most Rome prime districts.

Rome long-term yields run 2.5-3.5% in Prati on diplomatic and professional tenants. Compliant Trastevere STR achieves 3.5-5% gross with Jubilee 2026 occupancy tailwinds. Rome's income is more diversified; Naples' income is more tourism-concentrated.

## Centro Storico UNESCO: Naples vs Rome

Both cities constrain investors through UNESCO and Soprintendenza oversight, but enforcement flavour differs.

Naples centro storico spans Spaccanapoli, Decumani, and waterfront Castel dell'Ovo approaches. Facade alterations, archaeological discoveries during renovation, and seismic retrofit rules add cost and time. STR density scrutiny increases near Spaccanapoli tourist corridors.

Rome Municipio I covers Pantheon, Navona, and Trastevere edges with similar heritage vincoli plus abusi edilizi title risk. Rome's Jubilee infrastructure spend improves mobility; Naples' metro Line 6 expansion improves port-to-hill connectivity.

## Amalfi Access and Tourism Positioning

Naples' strategic advantage is southern Italy aggregation. Pompeii sits 30 minutes by train. Sorrento and Amalfi Coast drives start 1 hour south. Capri ferries depart from Molo Beverello. Cruise passengers overnight in Vomero and Chiaia before coast itineraries.

Properties marketed as "Naples plus Amalfi base" attract STR guests who want urban culture and coast day trips. Peak season runs April-October with Carnevale and Christmas spikes in centro.

Rome cannot replicate coast access but offers Vatican, Colosseum, and Jubilee pilgrimage demand at scale. Rome's tourism is larger in absolute visitors; Naples' tourism is more coast-linked.

## Risk Perception vs Operational Reality

Foreign media narratives still overweight Naples crime statistics from decade-old headlines. Operational reality in Vomero, Chiaia, and Posillipo differs: these hills host embassies, professional families, and rising foreign buyers priced out of Rome.

Rome benefits from lower perceived risk, more English-speaking property managers, and institutional familiarity among US and UK buyers. Absentee ownership is easier in Rome Trastevere with established expat networks.

Naples rewards investors who hire local geometra, campanian notaries, and on-the-ground managers who understand condominio politics and seismic certificates. Skipping local expertise magnifies risk beyond what headlines suggest.

Neither city forgives poor due diligence on conformità, but Naples' older building stock and seismic zone classification demand extra structural surveys.

## Liquidity and Exit Strategies

Rome prime resells in 4-8 months on clean-title Trastevere and Prati units during Jubilee cycles. Foreign enquiry growth of 44.7% in 2026 supports lifestyle buyer depth.

Naples Vomero and Chiaia sell to domestic professionals and growing foreign cohorts in 6-12 months when priced to comparables. Centro storico can take 9-15 months unless discounted for renovation backlog.

For 7-year exit horizons, Rome wins. For 12-year hold-with-income strategies, Naples yield compensates slower resale.

## Closing verification checklist

Use this Italy buyer checklist before compromesso signature or any wire to a notaio escrow account. Your independent avvocato should verify every line; the selling agent does not represent you at rogito.

**Identity and tax setup.** Confirm codice fiscale is issued and matches passport spelling exactly. Non-EU buyers need MAECI reciprocity confirmation in writing from the notaio file before deposit release. Open an Italian bank account or confirm your foreign bank can wire large euro sums with documented purpose codes.

**Title and cadastral consistency.** Request visura catastale storica and compare room counts, surface area, and category class against the on-site geometra survey. Mismatches between APE certificates, listing floor plans, and cadastral maps trigger registration delays and tax reassessments. Search for abusi edilizi (unauthorised works) on pools, terraces, and roof conversions; regularisation fines can exceed €10,000 on rural stock.

**Condominium and municipal debts.** Obtain certificato di regolarità contributi from the building administrator and verify no pending condominium lawsuits. Check IMU arrears and municipal service charges with the Comune; unpaid debts can transfer to the buyer after rogito.

**Rental and licensing.** If income matters, verify CIN (Codice Identificativo Nazionale) transferability or obtain a new CIN before marketing. Read condominium regolamento for tourist-use bans. Confirm SCIA or CIL filings for any structural work completed in the last five years. Model cedolare secca at 21% long-term or 26% short-term on gross rent, not net screenshots from listing portals.

**Closing economics.** Budget registration tax at 9% on second homes (2% only with prima casa and residency registration within 18 months). Add notary fees of 1% to 2%, agent commission if applicable, geometra survey, and mortgage bank costs. Wire only to the notaio conto provvisorio; never to seller personal accounts.

**Exit planning.** Italian plusvalenza may apply on sales within five years of purchase. Keep F24 payment receipts, renovation invoices, and rogito copies for capital gains calculations in Italy and your home country. If you hold through a company structure, confirm annual corporate filings and IVAFE exposure before year-end.

**Remote purchase.** Apostilled power of attorney must match the exact property description in compromesso. Allow two to three weeks for consular legalisation if buying from abroad. Schedule rogito only after mortgage perizia approval and abusi clearance are documented in writing.
