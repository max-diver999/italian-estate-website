Florence and Rome both sell Italian civilization to foreign buyers, but their investment maths diverge. Florence compresses demand into a walkable Renaissance core with aggressive STR regulation. Rome spreads demand across government, diplomacy, pilgrimage, and mass tourism with larger resale volume. Use the table before choosing a street.

## Property Prices Per Square Metre

Florence is Tuscany's pricing anchor and one of Italy's most expensive provincial cities on a per-metre basis. Aggregated portal data puts city averages near €4,737/m² in 2026, with Oltrarno, San Lorenzo, and Duomo-adjacent stock at €5,500-8,000/m² for restored palazzo floors.

Rome's headline index looks cheaper because suburban supply dilutes averages. Lazio residential comparators sit near €3,779/m², but prime Rome behaves separately. Trastevere, Prati, and Parioli palazzi range €3,500-6,500/m² depending on elevator, terrace, and conformità status, per our [Rome property investment guide](/guides/rome-property-investment-guide/).

### District price matrix

| Zone | Florence €/m² | Rome €/m² | Investor read |
|------|---------------|-----------|---------------|
| UNESCO historic core | €6,000-8,000 | €4,500-6,500 (Municipio I) | Florence premium on comparable heritage |
| STR-friendly fringe | €4,200-5,500 (Campo di Marte) | €4,000-5,500 (Trastevere fringe) | Rome offers more compliant STR paths |
| Value regeneration | €3,200-4,000 (Novoli) | €2,800-4,200 (Ostiense, EUR) | Rome lower entry on modern stock |
| Trophy address | €8,000+ (Arno-facing) | €6,500+ (Piazza-facing) | Both thin markets, long marketing |

On a 90 m² two-bedroom, Florence centro at €6,500/m² costs €585,000 before tax. Comparable Rome Trastevere at €5,000/m² costs €450,000 with higher STR compliance odds. Florence charges a prestige premium; Rome offers more metres per euro in prime districts.

## Art Tourism and Rental Demand

Both cities live on cultural tourism, yet demand shape differs.

Florence receives roughly 16-18 million annual visitors concentrated in a compact centro. Uffizi, Accademia, and Duomo queues drive short-stay apartment demand April through October. Average STR nightly rates on compliant two-bedrooms run €150-280 in peak season. Shoulder seasons thin without conference and government tenants to backfill.

Rome absorbs 30+ million visitors annually plus Jubilee 2026 pilgrimage surges. Vatican access, Colosseum timed entry, and state-sector employment create year-round footfall. Trastevere and Prati STR units achieve 65-75% peak occupancy in compliant setups; long-term diplomatic and university tenants stabilize winter months.

### Tourism demand comparison

| Metric | Florence | Rome |
|--------|----------|------|
| Annual visitors | ~16-18M | ~30M+ |
| Peak season share | April-October | March-November (Jubilee extended) |
| STR nightly (2BR peak) | €150-280 | €140-320 |
| Long-term tenant depth | Moderate (students, academics) | Deep (government, NGOs, universities) |
| 2026 special catalyst | Chianti wine tourism | Jubilee +44.7% foreign enquiries |

Investors who need winter income should weight Rome higher. Investors who accept seasonal peaks for Renaissance address prestige can model Florence on annualized STR spreadsheets with conservative occupancy.

## Rental Yields and Income Modelling

Neither city is Italy's yield champion. Compare both against southern regions in our [Italy rental yield guide](/guides/italy-rental-yield-guide/).

Rome gross yields span 2.5-3.5% on long-term residential in Prati and 3.5-5% on compliant STR in Trastevere when CIN, CIR, and condominium authorization align. Jubilee year may inflate gross projections; model net after IMU, management, platform fees, and 21% cedolare secca.

## UNESCO and Heritage Restrictions

UNESCO status protects value and constrains operations simultaneously.

Florence's centro storico within the historic walls faces some of Italy's strictest STR bans. New tourist rental registrations in dense zones are blocked; enforcement targets platforms listing non-compliant units. External alterations, window changes, and terrace additions require Soprintendenza approval with 6-18 month timelines.

Rome Municipio I applies density scrutiny, especially near Piazza Navona and Pantheon corridors, yet Trastevere and Prati retain clearer compliant STR paths when condominiums approve tourist use. Heritage vincoli still govern facades and archaeological zones. Abusi edilizi (unauthorized building works) create Rome-specific legal risk that Florence shares but with different enforcement patterns.

Our [heritage restricted property Italy guide](/guides/heritage-restricted-property-italy/) covers Soprintendenza process and renovation contingencies.

### Regulatory comparison

| Rule | Florence | Rome |
|------|----------|------|
| Centro STR new registrations | Banned in dense UNESCO core | Restricted, zone-dependent |
| CIN requirement | Yes nationwide | Yes nationwide |
| Condominium tourist-use vote | Critical | Critical |
| Renovation oversight | Soprintendenza strict | Soprintendenza + abusi risk |
| Typical renovation premium | 25-40% above standard | 25-45% above standard |

Buyers who depend on STR income should not assume Florence centro addresses remain legal because a listing existed historically. Rome offers more compliant inventory at similar prestige tiers.

## Foreign Buyer Mix and Demographics

Florence punches above its weight on foreign ownership concentration. American and British buyers lead art-tourism and academic purchases, followed by Germans and Swiss second-home hunters. Chinese and Gulf interest concentrates on trophy palazzo floors near the Arno. Foreign share on central transactions often exceeds 30% in peak years because the domestic pool is smaller than Rome.

Rome's market is larger and more Italian-weighted in outer municipi, yet central districts see rising foreign share driven by Jubilee marketing and lifestyle media. Americans, French, and British buyers lead international rogito counts in Trastevere and Prati. Institutional foreign volume still trails Milan, but Rome's absolute foreign transaction count exceeds Florence.

For sellers planning exit to international marketing, Rome's pool is deeper. Florence's pool pays premium for exact address prestige.

## Liquidity and Exit Strategies

Rome's residential market volume supports faster turnover on correctly priced stock in Trastevere, Prati, and EUR during Jubilee cycles. Expect 4-8 months on clean-title compliant units.

Florence centro apartments sell to lifestyle buyers and American art-world cohorts but investor buyer pools shrink when STR is banned. Well-priced Oltrarno and Campo di Marte units move in 6-10 months. Overpriced restored palazzi can sit 12-18 months.

Both cities punish skipped conformità and hidden abusi at exit when buyers' notaries re-run urbanistica checks.

## Decision Framework

### Choose Florence if:

- Renaissance address prestige is non-negotiable.
- You accept STR bans in centro and will buy in permitted zones or long-term let only.
- You plan 10-15 year hold with Chianti lifestyle use 4-6 weeks annually.
- Your buyer pool at exit is lifestyle-focused, not yield-focused.

### Choose Rome if:

- You want Jubilee tourism upside with broader year-round tenancy.
- You need compliant STR paths in Trastevere or Prati at lower €/m² than Florence centro.
- Capital-city liquidity and larger resale volume matter for 7-10 year hold.
- You prefer mixed long-term and short-term income strategies.

## Closing verification checklist

Use this Italy buyer checklist before compromesso signature or any wire to a notaio escrow account. Your independent avvocato should verify every line; the selling agent does not represent you at rogito.

**Identity and tax setup.** Confirm codice fiscale is issued and matches passport spelling exactly. Non-EU buyers need MAECI reciprocity confirmation in writing from the notaio file before deposit release. Open an Italian bank account or confirm your foreign bank can wire large euro sums with documented purpose codes.

**Title and cadastral consistency.** Request visura catastale storica and compare room counts, surface area, and category class against the on-site geometra survey. Mismatches between APE certificates, listing floor plans, and cadastral maps trigger registration delays and tax reassessments. Search for abusi edilizi (unauthorised works) on pools, terraces, and roof conversions; regularisation fines can exceed €10,000 on rural stock.

**Condominium and municipal debts.** Obtain certificato di regolarità contributi from the building administrator and verify no pending condominium lawsuits. Check IMU arrears and municipal service charges with the Comune; unpaid debts can transfer to the buyer after rogito.

**Rental and licensing.** If income matters, verify CIN (Codice Identificativo Nazionale) transferability or obtain a new CIN before marketing. Read condominium regolamento for tourist-use bans. Confirm SCIA or CIL filings for any structural work completed in the last five years. Model cedolare secca at 21% long-term or 26% short-term on gross rent, not net screenshots from listing portals.

**Closing economics.** Budget registration tax at 9% on second homes (2% only with prima casa and residency registration within 18 months). Add notary fees of 1% to 2%, agent commission if applicable, geometra survey, and mortgage bank costs. Wire only to the notaio conto provvisorio; never to seller personal accounts.

**Exit planning.** Italian plusvalenza may apply on sales within five years of purchase. Keep F24 payment receipts, renovation invoices, and rogito copies for capital gains calculations in Italy and your home country. If you hold through a company structure, confirm annual corporate filings and IVAFE exposure before year-end.

**Remote purchase.** Apostilled power of attorney must match the exact property description in compromesso. Allow two to three weeks for consular legalisation if buying from abroad. Schedule rogito only after mortgage perizia approval and abusi clearance are documented in writing.
