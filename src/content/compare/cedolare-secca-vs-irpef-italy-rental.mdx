Use this table before you register the first lease. Wrong election costs thousands over a 4+4 contract.

| Factor | Cedolare secca | Ordinary IRPEF |
|---|---|---|
| Tax base | Gross rent collected | Net rent after allowable deductions |
| Standard long-term rate | 21% | 23% to 43% progressive + regional add-ons |
| Subsidized concordato rate | 10% (eligible municipalities) | Same IRPEF brackets on net |

## What Is Cedolare Secca on Italian Rental Income?

Cedolare secca is an optional substitute tax on income from residential leases. Introduced to simplify landlord compliance, it replaces progressive IRPEF on that specific contract when you elect it at registration. The name means "dry coupon": a fixed percentage on gross rent with no deduction for IMU, condominium fees, insurance, repairs, or agency commissions.

The standard rate is 21% on free-market residential leases, including the first short-term tourist rental property. A reduced 10% rate applies only to concordato leases (typically 3+2 years) in municipalities that run subsidized rent programs. Short-term income from a second, third, or fourth STR property is taxed at 26% on gross bookings under cedolare secca, not 21%.

Election is per contract at registration with the Agenzia delle Entrate. You pay through the F24 form, usually quarterly or annually depending on how your commercialista structures remittance. Platforms like Airbnb may withhold 21% at source for non-residents, but you remain responsible for topping up to 26% on additional units or correcting misclassified income.

Cedolare secca does not replace IMU, tourist taxes, or condominium charges. It covers income tax only. That separation matters when you model net yield: a Milan apartment grossing €24,000 may pay €5,040 cedolare secca plus €1,200 to €2,000 IMU plus management, with none of those costs reducing the flat tax.

## How Does IRPEF Tax Italian Rental Income?

IRPEF (Imposta sul Reddito delle Persone Fisiche) is Italy's ordinary personal income tax. On rental income it applies to net rent after deductions permitted under TUIR rules: a flat 5% deduction on gross rent for unfurnished leases, or actual documented costs for furnished and short-term activity, plus IMU attributable to the rental period, maintenance, insurance, and mortgage interest within caps.

2026 national brackets on Italian-source income:

| IRPEF bracket | Taxable income band | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| First bracket | Up to €28,000 | 23% |
| Second bracket | €28,001 to €50,000 | 35% |
| Third bracket | Over €50,000 | 43% |

Regional and municipal surcharges add roughly 1.2% to 3.3% depending on region of tax residence. A tax resident in Lombardy with €60,000 of Italian rental net income can exceed 45% all-in marginal rate. A non-resident taxed only on Italian property income still uses the same brackets but may not owe regional surcharges in the same way; confirm with a commercialista.

IRPEF requires Modello Redditi PF (or inclusion in your resident return) and stronger bookkeeping. The upside is deductibility: a year with €18,000 gross rent, €4,000 IMU, €3,500 maintenance after a roof repair, and €2,000 management might net €8,500 taxable at 23%, roughly €1,955 tax versus €3,780 under 21% cedolare secca on gross. That is the classic IRPEF win scenario.

## Non-Resident vs Resident: Who Pays What?

Italy taxes Italian-source rental income where the property sits. A UK or US owner who stays tax resident abroad still owes Italian tax on rent from a Florence flat. A buyer who registers anagrafe and becomes Italian tax resident includes that rent in worldwide filing, but the cedolare secca versus IRPEF choice on the lease itself follows the same registration rules.

| Profile | Italian rental tax choice | Worldwide income impact |
|---|---|---|
| Non-resident foreign owner | Cedolare secca or IRPEF on Italian rent | Home country may tax same rent; treaty FTC often applies |
| Italian tax resident | Same election per lease | Rent stacks with salary, dividends, other Italian income |
| Article 24-bis flat tax resident | Cedolare secca or IRPEF on Italian rent | Italian rent outside €200k lump sum on foreign income |
| EU second-home owner | Same as non-resident if not anagrafe registered | Depends on home country residency |

Non-residents need an Italian codice fiscale and registered lease (RLI telematic filing). Some appoint a rappresentante fiscale when platforms do not withhold correctly. Residents must also track IVIE/IVAFE on foreign assets, irrelevant to most non-residents but critical after relocation.

## Short-Term vs Long-Term: Different Cedolare Rates

Contract length and guest stay pattern change both tax rate and compliance burden.

| Rental type | Stay length | Cedolare secca | IRPEF alternative | Extra compliance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard residential lease | 4+4 or 3+2 years | 21% (10% concordato) | Net IRPEF | RLI registration |
| Locazione breve, first property | Under 30 days per booking | 21% on gross | Net IRPEF | CIN mandatory, Alloggiati guest registry |
| Locazione breve, 2nd to 4th property | Under 30 days | 26% each | Net IRPEF | CIN per unit, platform reporting |
| Five or more STR units | Under 30 days | Business regime (VAT, P.IVA) | Corporate or IRPEF | SCIA, VAT accounting |

From 1 January 2026, managing three or more short-term properties triggers mandatory Partita IVA business registration under Decree Law 145/2023 reforms. Cedolare secca flat rates no longer apply at scale; activity is reclassified as impresa. Budget VAT at 10% on tourist lettings where applicable, plus social charges, before comparing to a single-unit 21% model.

Long-term leases avoid CIN but may produce lower gross rent in tourist zones. Short-term boosts gross but adds cleaning, platform fees, and municipal tourist taxes (€2 to €9.50 per guest night in major cities). Tax choice should follow net cash flow, not headline ADR. ## When Does IRPEF Beat Cedolare Secca?

IRPEF wins when net taxable rent times your marginal bracket falls below cedolare secca on gross rent. Break-even depends on bracket, deductions, and contract type.

Scenario 1: Low Italian income resident. Gross rent €14,000, IMU €1,100, management €1,400, maintenance €800. Net roughly €10,700 at 23% IRPEF equals €2,461. Cedolare secca at 21% on €14,000 equals €2,940. IRPEF saves about €480.

Scenario 2: Heavy capex year. Gross €22,000, deductible renovation €9,000, IMU €1,600, management €2,200. Net €9,200 at 23% equals €2,116. Cedolare at 21% on €22,000 equals €4,620. IRPEF saves over €2,500 in that year alone.

Scenario 3: High earner, simple lease. Gross €36,000, minimal deductions. Cedolare 21% equals €7,560. Net IRPEF near €34,000 hits 35% bracket, tax roughly €11,900 plus regional surcharge. Cedolare wins by wide margin.

Scenario 4: Concordato 10% cedolare. Subsidized rent caps gross at €9,600. Cedolare 10% equals €960. Net IRPEF after IMU might still exceed that if deductions are thin, but concordato cedolare is hard to beat on yield-focused social housing stock.

Rule of thumb for 2026: if your marginal IRPEF rate on net rent is 23% and deductions exceed 15% of gross rent, run IRPEF numbers before registering cedolare secca. If marginal rate is 35% or 43%, cedolare secca on gross almost always wins unless you run a multi-year renovation cycle and can time IRPEF elections on new contracts.

## What Documentation Do You Need?

Correct paperwork locks in your election and avoids reclassification fines.

| Step | Document / action | Who files | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Codice fiscale | Owner or consulate | Before purchase or lease |
| 2 | Ownership proof (rogito or visura catastale) | Notaio deed on file | At purchase |
| 3 | RLI lease registration | Landlord via Agenzia delle Entrate | Within 30 days of lease start |
| 4 | Cedolare secca election on RLI | Landlord | At registration |
| 5 | CIN (STR only) | Landlord via BDSR | Before first booking |
| 6 | Alloggiati Web guest reports (STR) | Landlord or manager | Within 24 hours of check-in |
| 7 | F24 tax payment | Commercialista | Quarterly or annual |
| 8 | Modello Redditi PF | Commercialista | If IRPEF path or mixed income |
| 9 | IMU declaration | Owner | Annual municipal filing |
| 10 | Platform withholding statements | Airbnb, Booking | Reconcile against F24 |

Keep scanned invoices for any IRPEF deduction claim. Cedolare secca audits focus on under-reported gross rent, especially where platform data sharing to Agenzia delle Entrate begins under EU DAC7-style rules in May 2026. Match booking exports to registered RLI income.

## How Do CIN and STR Rules Affect Your Tax Choice?

CIN (Codice Identificativo Nazionale) is not a tax regime. It is the national STR license code required for stays under 30 days, enforced from 2025 with full compliance by March 2026 for existing hosts. Missing CIN triggers fines of €800 to €8,000 independent of whether you paid 21% cedolare secca correctly.

Tax choice still matters alongside CIN:

- A licensed first STR unit at 21% cedolare secca beats IRPEF for most high-income non-residents.
- Adding a second STR unit jumps that property to 26% cedolare, narrowing the gap to IRPEF if expenses are high on the second unit.
- Crossing three STR properties exits private cedolare entirely; compare business VAT net to portfolio IRPEF with a commercialista before scaling.

Municipal layers stack on top: Florence restricts new STR in UNESCO zones, Milan requires SCIA, Rome uses SUAR registration. Tourist taxes must be collected and remitted even when platforms withhold income tax. Full regulatory map: [short-term rental rules in Italy](/guides/short-term-rental-rules-italy/).

Display CIN on every listing and at the property entrance. Tax auditors and tourism inspectors cross-reference BDSR databases with RLI income lines.

## Decision Framework: Choose Cedolare Secca If...

Elect cedolare secca when:

- Your marginal IRPEF rate on net rent would exceed 23%
- You want predictable quarterly cash tax without invoice tracking
- You operate standard long-term leases with moderate IMU and low maintenance volatility
- You are a non-resident with home-country tax already efficient and need simple Italian compliance
- You run one or two STR units at 21% or 26% and gross rent is strong relative to costs

Choose IRPEF when:

- Total Italian-source income stays in the 23% bracket after deductions
- You face a major renovation or insurance event that spikes deductible costs
- You hold concordato stock where rent is capped but 10% cedolare still loses to net IRPEF after IMU
- Your commercialista models multi-year IRPEF on a furnished long-term lease with heavy mortgage interest
- You are scaling past three STR units and business taxation requires net-based planning anyway

Hybrid portfolios are common: cedolare secca on a stable Milan 4+4 tenant, IRPEF on a Tuscany STR in a heavy-expense renovation year via a newly registered contract. You cannot hybridize on the same registered lease mid-term.

## Cedolare Secca vs IRPEF: Advantages and Disadvantages

| Path | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|---|---|
| Cedolare secca | Fixed rate on gross rent; simple F24 payments; predictable cash flow; strong for high earners | No IMU or cost deductions; 26% on extra STR units; lost if you scale to 3+ STR properties |
| IRPEF | Deduct IMU, maintenance, interest; can beat 21% in low-income or capex years | Progressive rates up to 43%; heavier Modello Redditi PF filing; audit risk on thin invoices |

Choose cedolare secca when simplicity and marginal rate protection matter. Choose IRPEF when documented expenses materially reduce net rent into the 23% bracket.
