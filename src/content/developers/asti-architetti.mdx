Paolo Asti founded Studio Asti Architetti in Milan after early experience at Gregotti Associati, building a practice focused on requalifying existing Milan buildings from the 1930s-1960s rather than defaulting to demolition. The studio serves banks, insurers, and real estate operators who treat city investments as long-horizon business opportunities with heritage compliance requirements.

OKAM Italy selected Asti Architetti as design architect on Naviglio Martesana residential conversions where industrial heritage sites transition to Class A and A4 apartment towers with biophilic terraces, concierge amenities, and canal-oriented landscaping marketed to international Milan buyers.

## Flagship Design: Prandina 34 Navigli

[Prandina 34 Naviglio Martesana](/projects/prandina-navigli-milan/) transforms a former industrial parcel into 30 Class A apartments across 3,500 sqm with Q2 2028 delivery target on OKAM Italy schedules. Asti design emphasizes soft architectural forms, light-filled interiors, fitness areas, co-working space, and concierge services supporting Navigli lifestyle marketing.

Project timetable published by OKAM lists start in Q3 2026 and delivery in Q2 2028 with unit mix spanning two, three, and four-bedroom layouts. Buyers benchmark pricing against Navigli Martesana comparables between €5,200 and €6,800 per sqm until official lists publish on okamitaly.it.

## Flagship Design: Meli 26 Navigli

[Meli 26 Navigli Martesana](/projects/meli-navigli-milan/) continues Asti's canal dialogue with 22 units across 2,700 sqm targeting Q1 2028 handover. Published specifications cite Class A4 energy rating with conciergerie, bike storage, fitness area, and co-working facilities aligned with OKAM amenity packages on Martesana corridor launches.

Meli positioning targets buyers needing earlier occupancy windows than Prandina's Q2 2028 flagship while sharing NAMA amphitheater and San Raffaele hospital proximity narratives in OKAM marketing materials.

## Heritage Retrofit Track Record

Beyond OKAM new-build commissions, Asti Architetti requalified historic Milan assets including ex Palazzo operations, Cortile della Seta restyling on Via Moscova, Torre Tirrena updates in Piazza Liberty, and restoration contexts around Torre Velasca heritage discussions. This track record matters for buyers evaluating whether Navigli industrial conversions receive design rigor comparable to centro storico retrofit projects.

Studio activity spans residential, office, and commercial sectors with integrated architectural design emphasis on buildings perceived as organic wholes rather than isolated facade treatments.
