International capital continued to flow into Italian residential real estate in 2025, with foreign buyers accounting for approximately **€5.5 billion in turnover**, according to Scenari Immobiliari figures cited by Milano Finanza. The total represents a **10% year-on-year increase** and reflects roughly **8,700 foreign families** purchasing at an average price near **€632,000**.

## Scenari Immobiliari reports double-digit growth in foreign turnover

The Scenari Immobiliari research unit, which tracks notarial registrations and buyer nationality trends, published its 2025 foreign-buyer summary through industry media including Milano Finanza. Analysts highlighted that international purchasers remained active even as domestic mortgage volumes fluctuated in the first half of the year.

The **€5.5 billion headline** captures completed residential transfers rather than preliminary offers or off-plan deposits. Scenari's methodology cross-checks Agenzia delle Entrate registration data with regional notary chambers, giving investors a reliable benchmark for cross-border capital flows.

Readers seeking full national context should cross-reference our [Italy real estate market data 2025](/guides/italy-real-estate-market-data-2025/) review, which integrates OMI transaction counts with foreign-buyer share estimates.

## Average ticket size rises to €632,000 per foreign family

Scenari Immobiliari calculated an average purchase price of approximately **€632,000** per foreign-buying household in 2025. The figure exceeds median domestic transaction values and reflects concentration in premium markets: Milan centro storico, Tuscan countryside estates, and coastal Puglia villas with pool and trullo restoration premiums.

Tecnocasa network commentary noted that American and British buyers often target turnkey properties with short-term rental history, while German and Swiss purchasers favour long-term lettings in Lombardy lake districts and South Tyrol periphery markets covered in our broader regional guides.

| Buyer segment | Approx. 2025 avg ticket | Typical property type |
| --- | --- | --- |
| US and UK lifestyle buyers | €550,000–€850,000 | Tuscan farmhouses, Puglia masserie |
| Milan corporate relocations | €700,000–€1.2M | Porta Nuova and Navigli apartments |
| Northern EU yield seekers | €400,000–€650,000 | Florence long-let units, Lucca flats |

## Regional hotspots: Tuscany prestige, Puglia value, Milan liquidity

Tuscany retained its appeal among international families seeking heritage properties and established short-term rental infrastructure. Our [Tuscany property investment guide](/guides/tuscany-property-investment-guide/) documents Florence asking prices near **€4,737 per square metre** and Chianti premium segments with slower but stable resale liquidity.

Puglia captured a growing share of foreign transactions, supported by Ostuni's national ranking for international enquiry and yields that often exceed northern gross returns. The [Puglia property investment guide](/guides/puglia-property-investment-guide/) outlines trulli restoration budgets, CIN licensing, and comune-level SCIA requirements.

Milan remained the liquidity anchor for buyers prioritising corporate tenant demand and regeneration upside in Porta Nuova, MIND, and Cascina Merlata. City asking prices averaged **€5,653 per square metre** in 2025 per Tecnocasa-linked indices cited across industry reports and in our [Milan property investment guide](/guides/milan-property-investment-guide/).

## Implications for 2026 acquisition strategy

The 10% turnover increase suggests foreign confidence held despite euro volatility and Italian tax debates on short-term rentals. Scenari analysts caution that average ticket inflation partly reflects mix shift toward larger rural properties rather than uniform price acceleration in every comune.

| Indicator | 2025 value | YoY change | Data source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Foreign residential turnover | €5.5 billion | +10% | Scenari / Milano Finanza |
| Foreign-buying families | ~8,700 | Stable to rising | Scenari Immobiliari |
| Average foreign purchase price | ~€632,000 | Up on mix | Scenari Immobiliari |
| National transactions (all buyers) | ~767,000 | +6.4–6.6% | OMI provisional |

Investors comparing regions should weigh gross yield against liquidity and regulatory risk. Puglia offers higher nominal yields but requires careful permit verification. Tuscany commands brand premium. Milan delivers depth for euro-denominated capital preservation with lower gross returns.

Gate-away portal data, published separately in 2025, shows enquiry volumes softening slightly year-on-year while five-year enquiry growth remains strong. Together, Scenari turnover figures and Gate-away search behaviour confirm that international interest in Italian property is broadening beyond legacy luxury postcodes into southern value markets.
