Italy's residential market closed 2025 with roughly **767,000 property transactions**, according to provisional figures published by the OMI through Scenari Immobiliari. The tally marks a **6.4% to 6.6% increase** over 2024 and signals that domestic mobility and international buyer demand held firm despite early-year mortgage uncertainty.

## OMI provisional data confirms rebound in transaction volumes

The Osservatorio del Mercato Immobiliare, Italy's official property observatory under the Agenzia del Territorio, released provisional 2025 residential transaction counts through the Scenari Immobiliari research network. The **767,000 figure** covers transfers of existing homes and new-build units registered through notarial deeds nationwide.

Scenari Immobiliari analysts noted that growth accelerated after the European Central Bank easing cycle reduced borrowing costs for Italian households. First-half volumes remained cautious, but the third and fourth quarters recovered momentum as sellers adjusted price expectations and buyers returned to urban employment centres.

For investors tracking macro trends, the updated OMI series aligns with the broader narrative in our [Italy real estate market data 2025](/guides/italy-real-estate-market-data-2025/) hub, which cross-references regional price indices and foreign-buyer turnover.

## Regional breakdown: Lombardy leads, Tuscany holds firm

Lombardy, anchored by Milan's corporate rental market, accounted for the largest share of national transactions. Tecnocasa network data cited in parallel industry reports show Milan maintaining liquidity in both resale apartments and regeneration-zone new supply.

Tuscany recorded stable to rising volumes in countryside communes popular with international second-home buyers. Lazio benefited from Rome's mixed domestic and tourism-linked demand. Southern regions, including Puglia, contributed a growing share of total deals as foreign enquiry converted into completed purchases, a trend detailed in our [Puglia property investment guide](/guides/puglia-property-investment-guide/).

## Cash buyers and mortgage-backed purchases both expanded

Industry observers report that cash transactions remained elevated in 2025, particularly among foreign purchasers and domestic sellers reinvesting equity from prior sales. Mortgage-backed deals recovered in the second half as banks offered more competitive fixed-rate products to qualified Italian residents.

Non-resident buyers continued to rely on cash or lower loan-to-value cross-border financing, a pattern consistent with the legal and tax framework outlined in our [buy property in Italy as a foreigner](/guides/buy-property-italy-foreigner/) guide. Notaries in Milan, Florence, and Ostuni reported shorter closing timelines when buyers arrived with full documentation and codice fiscale in place.

## What the 767,000 figure means for 2026 pricing

Transaction growth does not automatically translate into uniform price inflation. OMI price indices for 2025 show divergence: Milan asking values rose around **3.14%** to **€5,653 per square metre**, while several southern markets posted modest nominal gains with stronger yield profiles.

Analysts at Scenari Immobiliari expect 2026 volumes to remain supported if ECB rates stay stable and construction completions in Milan regeneration districts (Porta Nuova, MIND, Cascina Merlata) absorb corporate tenant demand. Investors should treat the 767,000 headline as a liquidity signal rather than a blanket buy signal across every comune.

The transaction rebound reinforces Italy's position as a dual-track market: northern cities offer depth and institutional tenant demand, while central and southern regions attract capital seeking lifestyle assets and higher gross yields.
