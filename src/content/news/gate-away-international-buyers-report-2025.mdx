## Gate-away 2025 headline metrics: softer year, strong half-decade trend

Gate-away, a leading portal for cross-border Italian property searches, published its 2025 international buyers report with nationality, regional, and budget breakdowns. Enquiry counts fell **5.89% year-on-year**, a moderation analysts link to higher European interest rates in early 2025 and delayed decision-making on lifestyle relocations.

Despite the annual dip, cumulative enquiry volume stands **65% above the five-year baseline**, confirming sustained international curiosity in Italian homes. Average enquiry value reached **€428,622**, below Scenari Immobiliari's completed-deal average near €632,000, which suggests many searchers target southern and central regions before upgrading to premium northern assets.

## United States leads nationality share; UK posts fastest growth

American buyers generated **25% of Gate-away Italy enquiries**, retaining the top nationality slot for another year. British enquiry volume rose **23.23% year-on-year**, the fastest growth rate among major source markets, reflecting post-Brexit relocation planning and sterling-euro arbitrage on Tuscan and Puglian countryside stock.

German, Dutch, and Swiss enquiries remained stable, while Nordic and Canadian search activity ticked higher on lake and coastal keywords. Gate-away analysts note that US and UK buyers increasingly filter for properties with existing CIN short-term rental registration, reducing time-to-income after notarial closing.

## Tuscany tops regions; Ostuni leads comune rankings

Regional data show **Tuscany capturing 14.77%** of Gate-away Italy enquiries, the highest regional share in 2025. Chianti, Val d'Orcia, and Florence periphery communes dominate keyword clusters tied to wine tourism and UNESCO heritage proximity.

At comune level, **Ostuni in Puglia ranked number one** nationally for international searches, extending a multi-year trend highlighted in local Tecnocasa and FIAIP commentary. Ostuni's white-city branding, Brindisi airport access, and established short-term rental market continue to attract US and UK capital.

## From enquiry to completion: what the data implies for agents and buyers

Gate-away enquiry declines do not contradict Scenari Immobiliari's report of **€5.5 billion foreign turnover** (+10% YoY). Search behaviour typically leads registered sales by two to four quarters. Buyers who enquired in late 2024 and early 2025 may still close in 2026 as notary queues clear and sellers accept revised pricing.

Average enquiry budgets near **€428,622** align with three-bedroom masserie in inland Puglia and restored casali in lower Chianti, rather than Milan Porta Nuova towers. Agents reporting longer qualification cycles should expect US and UK clients to request rental pro formas, cadastral compliance reports, and English-language compromesso review.

Milan enquiries remain a smaller Gate-away share by count but convert at higher ticket sizes when corporate relocations proceed.
