Milan's **living sector** attracted sustained institutional and private capital in 2025 as city-wide residential asking prices reached **€5,653 per square metre**, up **3.14% year-on-year**, according to Tecnocasa-linked market aggregates. Regeneration at **Porta Nuova**, **MIND**, and **Cascina Merlata** anchored new supply pipelines aimed at corporate tenants and long-term residents.

## Milan prices hold premium as national transactions rebound

Milan remained Italy's most expensive mainstream residential market in 2025, even as national OMI provisional data recorded roughly **767,000 transactions** nationwide (+6.4% to 6.6% YoY). Scenari Immobiliari analysts note that Lombardy led regional volume, with Milan accounting for a disproportionate share of foreign-buyer turnover reported at **€5.5 billion** nationally.

Prime centro storico and Porta Nuova units trade between **€4,500 and €7,500 per square metre**, while peripheral districts such as Rogoredo and San Donato offer lower entry points with stronger yield potential. Full district analysis appears in our [Milan property investment guide](/guides/milan-property-investment-guide/).

National price and volume context is consolidated in the [Italy real estate market data 2025](/guides/italy-real-estate-market-data-2025/) annual review.

## Living-sector capital targets regeneration corridors

The living sector, spanning build-to-rent, branded residences, and mixed-use towers, focused on three regeneration clusters in 2025:

**Porta Nuova** remains the benchmark for completed mixed-use stock, with Unicredit Tower area assets commanding premium rents from finance and consulting tenants. **MIND (Milan Innovation District)** continued phased residential delivery near Rho Fiera, attracting life-sciences and technology employers. **Cascina Merlata** expanded affordable and mid-market housing linked to San Siro transport nodes.

Tecnocasa agents report that foreign buyers represent roughly **22% of central Milan transactions**, led by EU, US, and Chinese capital seeking euro-denominated hard assets. Non-resident purchasers should budget 10% to 12% closing costs and confirm mortgage LTV limits outlined in our [buy property in Italy as a foreigner](/guides/buy-property-italy-foreigner/) guide.

| Regeneration zone | 2025 focus | Typical buyer profile |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Porta Nuova | Completed luxury stock, low vacancy | HNW, corporate relocations |
| MIND | Phased residential, innovation tenants | Mid-premium, long-let investors |
| Cascina Merlata | Mass housing, transport links | Yield-focused, domestic-heavy mix |

## Yields, regulation, and short-term rental constraints

Long-term gross yields in Milan run **3% to 5%**, below Puglia and Sicily holiday markets but supported by year-round demand from universities and multinational employers. Short-term rentals require national **CIN** registration plus Milan **SCIA** filing, with **€9.50 per person per night** tourist tax in 2026.

Investors comparing Milan with lifestyle markets should read the [Tuscany property investment guide](/guides/tuscany-property-investment-guide/), where Gate-away data show Tuscany leading regional enquiry share at **14.77%** but with UNESCO-linked STR restrictions in Florence. Yield-focused buyers often cross-check southern alternatives in the [Puglia property investment guide](/guides/puglia-property-investment-guide/).

## 2026 outlook for Milan living-sector investors

Analysts expect Milan asking prices to track low-single-digit nominal growth if ECB rates remain stable and corporate hiring in Lombardy continues. Living-sector funds may accelerate acquisitions in MIND and remaining Scali Ferroviari parcels as construction risk clears.

Foreign turnover growth of **10%** nationally suggests Milan will retain share among buyers prioritising liquidity over maximum yield. Pair city exposure with southern diversification only after modelling net returns post **cedolare secca** tax and IMU on second homes.

Scenari Immobiliari and Tecnocasa commentary converge on one point: Milan is a capital-preservation and tenant-depth play, not a high-yield frontier. Regeneration names (Porta Nuova, MIND, Cascina Merlata) belong in any serious Lombardy thesis, but entry timing and perizia appraisal gaps still determine whether a specific unit clears investor hurdles.
