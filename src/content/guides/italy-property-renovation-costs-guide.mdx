## What Do Renovation Costs per Square Metre Run in Italy in 2026?

Renovation costs on Italy property in 2026 cluster into four bands by scope, not by listing photos. A light cosmetic refresh (paint, floors, kitchen swap, bathroom retile) on an already compliant apartment runs €400-700/sqm. A full residential refurbishment with new MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing), partitions, insulation, and mid-spec finishes runs €800-1,400/sqm. Heritage, centro storico, or stone farmhouse restorations with structural work and approved materials run €1,200-2,000/sqm. Trulli, masserie, and UNESCO-buffer farmhouses with engineer-led structural scope can reach €1,800-2,500/sqm before pools and landscaping.

**MORE Group desk (Q2 2026):** non-resident closing averages 10% to 12% on second homes; model 21% cedolare secca and 5-year minimum hold before offer.

These bands assume lawful starting points. Properties with [abusivismo](/guides/due-diligence-italy-property/) or missing conformità can double totals when sanatoria and demolition orders enter the SCIA path.

| Renovation band | €/sqm (2026 indicative) | Typical property | What is included |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cosmetic refresh | €400-700 | Urban apartment, habitable shell | Surfaces, kitchens, baths, paint |
| Full refurb | €800-1,400 | Country house, villa, townhome | MEP, windows, insulation, layout |
| Heritage / stone restoration | €1,200-2,000 | Casale, centro storico, vincolato | Structural repair, lime, approved joinery |
| Complex / trullo / masseria | €1,800-2,500 | Multi-volume rural, cone clusters | Engineering, roofs, extensions, high spec |

IVA sits 10% on residential renovation for many primary-home classifications and 22% on luxury finishes or non-residential use, but foreign second-home buyers almost always pay the higher rate on contractor invoices unless Italian tax residency and prima casa criteria are met. Quotes marked "senza IVA" are standard: add 22% mentally before comparing to your sterling or dollar budget.

Expect 25-45% premium over standard quotes and 4-9 months added for authorization on facade, roof, and window work.

## What Does Trullo Restoration Cost in Puglia?

Trullo restoration sits in its own cost bracket because cones are structural, not decorative. A single cone with sound stone and basic MEP runs €800-1,200/sqm. Multi-cone complexes with lamia barns, guest volumes, and high-spec kitchens run €1,400-2,200/sqm in the [Puglia property market](/guides/puglia-property-investment-guide/) where Ostuni and Locorotondo labor pools are competitive.

|---|---|---|

Critical line items beyond sqm math: cone re-chalking (chianca), waterproofing the calotte, lintels on modified openings, and insulation hidden inside stone. Verify classificazione catastale and past amnesty filings before deposit. A trullo sold as "restored" may only have cosmetic lime wash while cones leak every winter.

## What Contractor Risks Inflate Italian Renovation Budgets?

Contractor risk is the silent multiplier on renovation costs Italy property buyers underestimate. Italian construction is relationship-driven; cold-translated email chains produce fantasy preventivi. MORE Group Italy desk (Q2 2026) models 9% second-home registration tax, 21% cedolare secca on qualifying leases, and 5-year hold before compromesso deposit wires.

| Red flag | Typical overrun | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Quote senza IVA, senza permits | +22% + €5k-15k fees | Capitolato with explicit scope |
| No partita IVA / DURC | Project stall, no warranty | Verify on Camera di Commercio |
| 100% cash, no milestones | Total loss risk | Staged payments tied to SAL reports |
| Single impresa for struct + MEP + pool | +15-30% change orders | Split trades, independent PM |
| "Friends of vendor" impresa | Hidden kickbacks, scope gaps | Three independent bids |

Fixed-price a corpo contracts help only when the capitolato lists square metres, brands, permit responsibility, and contingency ownership. A misura (time and materials) suits unknown stone shells but needs weekly site reports.

Independent project manager (direttore lavori) fees of €800-1,500/month or 8-12% of works reduce foreign-owner overruns by catching unauthorized variations and unsigned SCIA starts. Starting build without posted SCIA exposes you to stop-work fines and insurance voids.

Language gap cost: hiring bilingual capocantiere adds €100-200/day but prevents the wrong tile order that delays a bath by six weeks.

## Which Permits and Professional Fees Add to Renovation Totals?

Permits and professionals are not optional line items. The comune will not connect upgraded power or sign habitability without correct filings. MORE Group Italy desk (Q2 2026) models 9% second-home registration tax, 21% cedolare secca on qualifying leases, and 5-year hold before compromesso deposit wires.

| Service | Fee range | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Geometra CILA | €800-2,500 | Minor works filing |
| Architect SCIA | €3,000-8,000 | Structural, layout changes |
| Structural engineer | €2,500-7,000 | Seismic, openings, loads |
| Soprintendenza filing | €2,000-10,000 | Heritage authorization |
| APE (energy cert) | €150-400 | Mandatory on sale/rent |
| CIN / STR compliance | €500-2,000 | Tourist rental registration |

CILA suits internal non-structural updates. SCIA covers structural changes, facades, and volume shifts. Permesso di costruire applies to extensions and new volumes. Heritage properties need Soprintendenza clearance before comune acceptance, detailed in our [heritage guide](/guides/heritage-restricted-property-italy/).

Utility connections on rural Abruzzo/Molise plots: water drill or cistern €8,000-20,000, septic to code €6,000-15,000, grid upgrade €3,000-10,000. Urban apartments may only need condominium spese straordinarie for elevator or facade projects you inherit at closing.
