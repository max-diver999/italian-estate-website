## What is the Amalfi Coast property market doing in 2026?

The Costiera Amalfitana occupies a narrow strip of Campania coastline between Salerno and the Sorrento peninsula, anchored by towns that global tourism has branded for decades: Positano, Amalfi, Ravello, and the quieter cliff villages of Praiano, Furore, and Conca dei Marini. For property investors, the coast behaves like a hybrid of Lake Como's scarcity pricing and Tuscany's tourism rental logic, but with sharper seasonality, steeper geotechnical risk, and heavier landscape regulation because the entire corridor sits inside a UNESCO World Heritage landscape.

National benchmarks frame the premium. Italy's average residential asking price sat near €2,188/m² in April 2026 comparators. Campania regional averages run below that headline, yet Amalfi Coast prime towns ignore regional indices entirely. Positano sea-view apartments and villa fractions routinely list at €6,500-10,000/m². Praiano and Conca dei Marini offer relative value at €4,500-6,500/m². Ravello hilltop properties with terrace panoramas command €5,000-9,000/m² depending on garden size and parking access.

Foreign capital continues to enter Italy at scale. Scenari/Nomisma data for 2025 records 8,700 foreign-family transactions and €5.5 billion in turnover nationally. The Amalfi Coast captures a slice of the upper lifestyle tier rather than volume yield investors. Gate-away enquiry patterns tracked in Italian Estate desk research cluster Anglo-Saxon and American buyers around Positano, Ravello, and Sorrento gateway searches, often comparing the coast against Tuscany, Capri, and Puglia in the same session.

The investment question is not whether the Amalfi Coast is recognizable. It is whether your mandate matches what the coast rewards: capital preservation, personal use, and seasonal rental income with rigorous compliance, rather than year-round cash flow at Puglia or Sicily yield bands.

Positano sets the coast's price ceiling for apartments with verified sea views and compliant terraces. Praiano attracts buyers priced out of Positano who still want cliffside views and boat beach access without Positano's congestion premium.

## What drives Positano property investment demand?

Positano is the coast's global postcard. American and British buyers appear repeatedly in agent enquiry logs for two-bedroom sea-view apartments in the €800,000-1.8 million band and villa fractions above €2 million. The town's inventory is constrained by topography: buildable land is limited, height restrictions apply under landscape vincolo, and new supply arrives mostly through renovation of existing volumes rather than greenfield development.

Investment strengths include internationally recognized branding, premium nightly STR rates in peak season, and resilient resale demand from lifestyle buyers even when yield math is thin. A compliant two-bedroom with terrace and sea view can achieve €350-650 nightly in July-August on managed platforms before fees, but annual occupancy outside peak months drops sharply.

Investment frictions are equally concrete. Parking is expensive and scarce; luggage logistics for guests affect reviews. Condominium rules increasingly restrict short-term letting in central clusters. Landslide and retaining-wall maintenance costs run higher than flat coastal markets. Any terrace extension or pool project triggers Soprintendenza pathways described in our [heritage restricted property Italy](/guides/heritage-restricted-property-italy/) guide.

Positano suits buyers who underwrite 2.5-4% gross long-term or 3.5-5% gross STR with professional management, accept 10-15% closing costs, and plan holds of seven years or longer. It does not suit investors seeking Puglia-style 6-8% gross on modest entry tickets.

## How does Amalfi town compare for investors?

Amalfi is the historic maritime republic centre with a working town rhythm beyond tourism peaks. Pricing runs below Positano on equivalent square metres but heritage density in the centro storico is high. Cathedral proximity, harbour frontage, and valley-side apartments each represent different risk-return profiles.

Harbour-front and central apartments attract STR operators targeting culture tourists and ferry-linked itineraries along the coast. Gross yields of 3-4.5% appear in managed STR models when CIN and CIR are in place and condominium bylaws permit affitti brevi. Long-term residential demand from local professionals and hospitality staff supports 2.5-3.5% gross on valley-side units away from peak noise.

Amalfi buyers must scrutinize flood and hydrological history near the river mouth, a recurring maintenance theme after heavy Campania rainfall. Access during SS163 closures after landslides can pause guest check-ins for days. Verify alternative pedestrian routes and property manager contingency plans before modelling STR income.

For foreign purchase mechanics, the legal pathway matches the rest of Italy: notary-led closing, 9% registration tax on second homes, and independent legal counsel. ## Why do investors target Ravello and Praiano?

Ravello sits above the cliff line at roughly 365 metres elevation, offering panoramic terraces and a quieter residential character than Positano's dense vertical fabric. The town hosts high-end hospitality and cultural events, including the Ravello Festival, that support premium short-stay pricing in summer. Villa inventory with gardens and parking trades at €5,000-9,000/m² with gross yields often in the 2.5-4% band because purchase prices are high relative to winter demand.

Ravello investors frequently target dual-use properties: owner occupation in spring and autumn, managed STR in peak summer, occasional event-weekend premiums. Heritage constraints on historic palazzi are stringent; budget 25-45% renovation premium when Soprintendenza oversight applies.

Praiano functions as Positano's value-adjacent neighbour on the same coastline. Sea-view apartments often list 15-25% below Positano equivalents while sharing similar boat-beach access and SS163 connectivity. Praiano suits yield-aware lifestyle buyers who still want cliffside positioning but reject Positano entry tickets above €1.2 million for modest two-bedroom stock.

Insider tip from our Campania files: Praiano managers report stronger repeat-booking rates than Positano for family STR guests who prioritize beach clubs and quiet evenings over Positano's nightlife density. Underwrite slightly lower nightly rates but higher shoulder-season occupancy when modelling Praiano versus Positano for the same bedroom count.

## How extreme is short-term rental seasonality on the coast?

Seasonality is the defining cash-flow variable. Managed operators in Positano and Praiano routinely report that July and August produce 45-55% of annual gross STR revenue. April through June and September through October contribute another 30-35% combined. November through March often falls below 20% occupancy unless the unit is marketed to long-stay remote workers, winter sun seekers, or Ravello event attendees.

| Month band | Revenue share (typical STR) | Operational notes |
|---|---|---|
| Jan-Mar | 5-12% | Maintenance window, deep discounts |
| Apr-Jun | 18-22% | Shoulder pricing, weather variance |
| Jul-Aug | 45-55% | Peak rates, max congestion |
| Sep-Oct | 12-18% | Strong shoulder, weather dependent |
| Nov-Dec | 5-10% | Event niches in Ravello only |

Investors who model 280 occupied nights at peak pricing consistently overstate returns. Underwrite 120-140 rentable nights for Positano two-bedroom stock unless the property includes parking, elevator access, and a proven multi-year review history above 4.8 stars.

Regulatory compliance is non-negotiable. Italy requires a CIN for all short-term listings. Campania adds CIR registration. Positano and Amalfi municipalities actively enforce against unlicensed operators. Professional hosts with three or more properties face SCIA and business registration requirements from 2026. ## What UNESCO and vincolo paesaggistico rules apply?

The Costiera Amalfitana has been UNESCO World Heritage since 1997. Practically, that status reinforces landscape protection under D.Lgs 42/2004. Coastal bands, cliffside terraces, and viewsheds within protected corridors trigger vincolo paesaggistico review for external works, pools, terrace expansions, roof modifications, and often window replacements facing public vistas.

Buyers who assume internal kitchen and bathroom upgrades are always free-form discover otherwise when a vaulted ceiling, shared facade, or retaining wall triggers Soprintendenza involvement. Unauthorized work carries fines from €1,000 to €156,000 and remediation orders without time limits in many cases.

Before compromesso, order a certificato di vincolo and have a geometra cross-check municipal PRG zoning against Soprintendenza archives. Our [heritage restricted property Italy](/guides/heritage-restricted-property-italy/) guide details approval timelines of 4-9 months for heritage interventions versus 6-10 weeks on unrestricted stock.

On the Amalfi Coast, new buildable volume is rare. Value creation flows through restoration of existing cubic capacity rather than expansion. That supports long-term scarcity but increases renovation cost uncertainty.

## What landslide and access risks must buyers verify?

Campania's geology makes frana, landslide, risk a first-order due diligence item, not a survey footnote. Steep slopes above SS163, terrace retaining walls, and cliffside roads require professional geotechnical review before binding purchase.

| Risk factor | Where it appears | Due diligence action |
|---|---|---|
| Active landslide zones (PAI) | Cliff towns, SS163 cuts | Geologist relazione geologica |
| Retaining wall failure | Terrace villas | Structural engineer inspection |
| Road closure (SS163) | Entire coast | Manager contingency plans |
| Parking scarcity | Positano, Amalfi centro | Deeded space or lease proof |
| Flood exposure | Amalfi valley mouth | Hydrological history review |
| Drainage neglect | Older terraces | Capex budget 8-12% annual |

Request the property's PAI classification from municipal planning offices. Verify maintenance records for retaining walls and drainage channels. Confirm legal access rights if the unit relies on shared steps or private lanes, common in Positano and Furore.

Properties marketed as "walk to beach" may require hundreds of vertical steps that affect guest demographics and luggage logistics. Factor that into STR target markets and review scores.

## How does Sorrento work as a gateway market?

Sorrento sits on the northern Sorrentine peninsula, outside the UNESCO Amalfi corridor but functionally linked by ferry, bus, and private transfer routes. For international buyers, Sorrento often serves as the first base during inspection trips: faster road access from Naples Capodichino airport, richer year-round services, and pricing 20-35% below Positano for comparable apartment sizes.

Gateway logic matters for portfolio construction. Some investors purchase in Sorrento for personal use and rental with stronger winter occupancy from Naples-linked tourism, while keeping a smaller Positano or Praiano unit for peak-season premium STR. Others reverse the sequence: stage due diligence from Sorrento hotels, then commit capital to Amalfi towns once geotechnical and vincolo checks clear.

Train links via Circumvesuviana connect Sorrento to Naples, supporting domestic tourism and long-stay renters who avoid driving SS163. Ferry links reach Capri, Positano, and Amalfi directly in season. Compare against Capri's ferry-only access and island premium pricing before deciding where scarcity justifies lower yields.

American buyers typically arrive with all-cash or low-LTV financing, targeting Positano or Ravello two-bedroom terraces in the €900,000-2.2 million range. Motivations mix legacy family use, euro diversification, and Instagram-visible lifestyle assets. They often accept 2.5-3.5% gross yields if personal use covers six to ten weeks annually.

## What due diligence steps matter before closing?

Amalfi Coast transactions punish skipped verification more than flat urban markets. Execute the full checklist in our [due diligence Italy property](/guides/due-diligence-italy-property/) guide plus coast-specific steps:

1. Order certificato di vincolo and Soprintendenza archive search before compromesso.
2. Commission geotechnical relazione geologica and retaining-wall structural review on slope properties.
3. Verify CIN and CIR status for STR intent; confirm condominium bylaws in writing.
4. Inspect drainage, terrace waterproofing, and cliff-edge guardrails with local engineer.
5. Confirm deeded parking or valid lease; Positano parking adds €30,000-80,000 value.
6. Model SS163 closure contingency for STR check-in logistics.
7. Review abusi edilizi and conformità urbanistica, terrace enclosures are common legacy issues.

Engage bilingual legal counsel independent of the selling agency. Campania commission structures often split between buyer and seller; conflicted advice is common when the same network represents both sides.
