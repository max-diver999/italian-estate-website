## How should you read the 2026 city yield ranking?

Start with gross yield only as a screening filter, then model net cash flow before comparing tickets across communes. Portal listings in Italy almost always quote gross returns on purchase price, excluding IMU property tax, condominium spese, realistic vacancy, management commissions of 10-30%, and rental income tax under [cedolare secca](/guides/cedolare-secca-italy-rental-property/) or ordinary IRPEF. A Palermo apartment advertised at 8% gross can net near 5.5-6% after disciplined costs, while a Milan Navigli unit at 4.5% gross may net under 3% once IMU and agency fees land.

## How do gross and net yields differ across these twelve cities?

Gross yield is annual rent divided by purchase price. Net yield subtracts operating costs and income tax. The gap is not uniform. High-IMU, high-condominium northern stock compresses net returns more than southern apartments where gross spreads are wider but compliance risk is higher.

| Cost line | Milan | Rome / Florence | Bologna / Naples | Bari / Lecce / Ostuni | Palermo / Catania / Syracuse / Matera |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|------------------|----------------------|---------------------------------------|
| IMU (typical second home) | 0.5-0.76% cadastral | 0.76-1.06% | 0.76-1.06% | 0.86-1.06% | 0.86-1.06% |
| Condominium (monthly) | €120-350 | €100-300 | €80-250 | €60-180 | €50-150 |
| Management LTR | 10-12% rent | 10-15% | 10-15% | 10-15% | 12-18% |
| Management STR | 20-28% revenue | 22-30% | 22-30% | 25-35% | 25-35% |
| Maintenance reserve | 1.0-1.5%/yr | 1.0-2.0%/yr | 1.0-1.8%/yr | 1.2-2.0%/yr | 1.5-2.5%/yr |
| Typical gross-to-net gap | 1.5-2.0 pts | 1.5-2.5 pts | 1.5-2.3 pts | 1.3-2.0 pts | 1.5-2.5 pts |

Investors who elect [cedolare secca](/guides/cedolare-secca-italy-rental-property/) cannot deduct IMU or management against that income, which makes net modeling especially important in Milan and Florence where fixed costs are large relative to rent. Ordinary IRPEF with deductions sometimes wins on heavily leveraged or high-expense renovations; run both paths with a commercialista before rogito.

Worked example (Ostuni two-bedroom, €320,000 purchase): €22,400 annual STR gross (7.0%), minus €6,720 management at 30%, €2,400 IMU, €3,200 maintenance and utilities, equals €10,080 pre-tax cash. Cedolare secca at 21% on first qualifying property STR path or 26% on second property removes €2,117-€2,621, leaving €7,459-€7,963 net, or roughly 2.3-2.5% net on price unless you bought below ask. This is why [gross vs net yield Italy](/guides/gross-vs-net-yield-italy/) insists on full-stack modeling, not portal headlines.

## Should you choose short-term or long-term rental by city?

Long-term leases (4+4 or 3+2 contracts) stabilize income in university and business cities. National rules require CIN registration for all STR; see [short-term rental rules Italy](/guides/short-term-rental-rules-italy/).

## What yields should you expect in northern Italy: Milan and Bologna?

Milan remains Italy's liquidity anchor. City-wide asking prices averaged near €5,653/m² in early 2026 with prime centro at €4,500-7,500/m² per our [Milan property investment guide](/guides/milan-property-investment-guide/). Long-term gross yields run 2.5-4.5% in Brera and Duomo, 4-5% in Navigli, Isola, and Bocconi corridors. STR gross can exceed 5% where SCIA and CIN are clean, but tourist tax and management erode advantage unless you self-manage.

Bologna combines Emilia-Romagna stability with Europe's oldest university footprint. Asking prices of €2,400-3,800/m² deliver 3.5-5.0% long-term gross near Porta Saragozza and the high-speed station, with STR summer uplift on furnished units. Bologna suits investors who want northern governance and tenant depth without Milan entry tickets. Compare Emilia context in [best regions for Italy property investment 2026](/guides/best-regions-invest-italy-property-2026/).

Northern underwriting tip: model IMU on cadastral value, not asking price. Lombardy and Emilia-Romagna surcharges push effective IMU toward the upper band on second homes, which widens the gross-net gap relative to portal quotes.

## How do Rome and Florence balance tourism yield and regulation?

Rome asking prices span €2,600-4,500/m² in investable districts, with gross long-term yields of 3-5% and STR gross of 4.5-6.5% where Jubilee 2026 tourism spillover holds. EUR, Prati, and well-connected suburbs offer steadier LTR; historic-centre STR faces SUAR registration and condominium restrictions that can block licenses entirely.

Florence trades UNESCO prestige for tight STR caps. Centro storico gross STR can look attractive on paper at 5-6.5%, but license quotas and condominium veto rights make net outcomes volatile. Peripheral Florence and Scandicci deliver 4-5% long-term gross with fewer STR headaches. Both cities punish buyers who skip license verification before compromesso.

For national tax context on furnished leases versus tourist lets, cross-read the [Italy rental yield guide](/guides/italy-rental-yield-guide/) section on cedolare secca bands before you assume STR always beats LTR on a net basis.

## Why do Naples and Bari sit in the mid-yield tier?

Naples offers €1,650-2,800/m² entry in viable districts with 4-5.5% long-term gross and STR spikes in centro storico when licenses exist. Tenant demand is deep, but building compliance, condominium politics, and regional bureaucracy raise friction scores. Vomero and Fuorigrotta suit LTR; Chiaia and centro suit STR with legal diligence.

Bari, Puglia's administrative capital, combines Adriatic port growth with Policlinico-linked rental demand. Murattiano and Madonnella deliver 5-6.5% long-term gross at lower tickets than Milan. STR works on summer coastal spillover; LTR fits medical and university tenants year-round. Bari bridges yield-focused south and institutional north: better infrastructure than deep Sicily, higher gross than Bologna on equivalent capital.

## Which Puglia cities lead foreign-buyer yield: Ostuni and Lecce?

Ostuni ranked among Italy's most searched communes by international buyers for two consecutive years in FIAIP commentary cited in our [Puglia property investment guide](/guides/puglia-property-investment-guide/). White-city pool villas at €320,000-480,000 often achieve 6-8.5% STR gross in peak season, 4.5-6% long-term gross on restored masserie. Net outcomes hinge on pool conformità, CIN transfer at sale, and winter occupancy of 35-50% unless you have booking history.

Lecce baroque centre trades €1,400-2,400/m² with STR gross of 5.5-7.5% and long-term gross near 4.5-6%. Salento airport access supports tourism, while local LTR demand anchors off-season cash flow better than pure hill-town plays. Ostuni suits pool-villa STR; Lecce suits centro apartments with lower maintenance than rural trulli.

## Do Palermo, Catania, and Syracuse offer Italy's highest city gross yields?

Sicily's major cities lead gross bands among established communes because entry prices remain low relative to rent. Palermo revival districts (Kalsa, Libertà, Mondello fringe) show 5.5-7.5% long-term gross and 7-9.5% STR gross on disciplined tickets. Catania benefits from Etna tourism and 60,000+ student enrollment, with gross yields similar to Palermo and strong furnished LTR near the university.

Syracuse, especially Ortigia, commands STR premiums with 6-8.5% gross when licenses and heritage restrictions align. LTR depth is thinner; income is seasonal. All three require strict abusivismo checks, earthquake-zone compliance on older stock, and management partners who collect rent reliably. Gross leadership does not mean passive ownership: Sicily rewards hands-on underwriting.

## Where does Matera fit in a yield portfolio?

Matera Sassi cave hotels and restored palazzi can achieve 6-8% STR gross, among the highest tourism multiples in southern Italy. Heritage law, fire safety upgrades, and limited LTR demand make Matera a specialist STR play, not a balanced income city. Thin resale liquidity outside peak tourism seasons means hold periods should exceed five years unless you buy with existing CIN and booking track record.

Net yields after restoration amortization often lag Ostuni pool villas unless you acquired Sassi stock before 2023 price run-ups. Matera belongs in a diversified Italy portfolio as a tourism kicker, not the sole yield anchor.

### Scenario C: Corporate LTR stability (Milan, Rome EUR, Bologna)

Optimal path: Furnished twelve-month contracts to corporate or hospital tenants; avoid unlicensed STR in regulated historic zones.

Expected outcome: Milan 3% to 5% gross, net 2% to 3.5%; Rome EUR similar with SUAR compliance on any short stays. Capital preservation and financing depth beat southern spread at this mandate.
