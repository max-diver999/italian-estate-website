# Cadastral Value vs Market Price Italy: 2026 Tax Guide

A €950,000 apartment in Milan's Navigli district might carry a valore catastale of €180,000. That gap is not an error, it is how Italy taxes property. International buyers who model purchase tax on the advertised price routinely overstate their liability on private resales and under-budget annual IMU on second homes. This guide explains cadastral value Italy property rules for 2026: how rendita catastale becomes valore catastale, why the state prefers cadastral bases, how prezzo-valore interacts with registration tax and capital gains, and what to verify on a visura catastale before you wire a deposit.

## What is cadastral value in Italian property law?

Cadastral value (valore catastale) is the statutory tax base assigned to every registered building in Italy. It is derived from rendita catastale, a theoretical annual rental income set by the land registry, not from the price a buyer and seller agree in the market.

Every parcel and unit in Italy sits in the Catasto, administered by Agenzia delle Entrate. When officials register a residential apartment, they assign a categoria catastale (for example A/2 for a standard flat or A/8 for a villa) and a rendita catastale expressed in euros per year. That rendita is not what you could charge on Airbnb; it is a table-driven figure linked to size, location band, and building quality at the time of classification or last survey.

Market price reflects supply, finishes, view, and buyer sentiment. Valore catastale reflects administrative tables. In prime cities the ratio often ranges from 1:5 to 1:10, meaning taxes calculated on cadastral value can be a fraction of what you would pay if levies tracked open-market transactions. The divergence is deliberate. Lawmakers wanted a stable, auditable base that does not require annual renegotiation with taxpayers and that resists under-declaration of sale prices.

For purchase planning, treat valore catastale as the number your notary multiplies by 2% or 9% when you buy from a private seller under prezzo-valore. For ownership planning, the same base feeds IMU calculations on non-exempt homes. Confusing cadastral value with market price is one of the most common budgeting mistakes among first-time buyers in Italy.

## How is rendita catastale converted to valore catastale?

The conversion uses a fixed revaluation factor of 1.05 applied to rendita catastale, then a category multiplier set by law. For most residential purchases the multiplier is 120 for second homes and 110 for prima casa, producing effective factors of 126 and 115.5 respectively.

The formula written in notarial deeds is:

**Valore catastale = rendita catastale × 1.05 × multiplier**

The 1.05 coefficient revalues cadastral income to reflect statutory adjustment. The multiplier depends on property use at registration:

| Use at registration | Statutory multiplier | Effective factor (× 1.05) | Example rendita €1,500 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Second home / non-resident | 120 | 126 | Valore catastale €189,000 |
| Prima casa (eligible) | 110 | 115.5 | Valore catastale €173,250 |
| Offices (A/10) | 60 | 63 | Valore catastale €94,500 |
| Shops (C/1) | 40.8 | 42.84 | Valore catastale €64,260 |
| Cellars / warehouses (C/2) | 120 | 126 | Valore catastale €189,000 |

Consider a Tuscan farmhouse listed at €780,000 with rendita catastale €1,200 on the visura. A non-resident buyer calculates: €1,200 × 1.05 × 120 = €151,200 valore catastale. Registration tax at 9% equals €13,608 before the €1,000 minimum, versus €70,200 if tax ran on full market price. The arithmetic is simple; the discipline is obtaining the correct rendita before you sign.

Commercial categories use lower multipliers because rendita tables already embed higher nominal yields. Annex units (garages C/6, cellars C/2) linked to a main home can follow residential multipliers when correctly bound in the deed; standalone accessory units taxed at 9% on their own rendita catch many buyers off guard. Cross-check annex classifications in the [cost of buying property in Italy](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-italy/) breakdown before you model total acquisition spend.

## Why do Italian property taxes use cadastral value instead of market price?

Italy shifted most transfer taxes to cadastral bases to stop purchase-price under-reporting and give buyers legal certainty. Under the prezzo-valore system introduced in 2006, Agenzia delle Entrate generally cannot reassess a private resale based on a higher market price if tax was paid on valore catastale.

Before 2006, registration tax on private sales often used declared price, which encouraged parallel cash payments and deed prices below reality. Tax audits followed, with penalties and interest. Parliament replaced that uncertainty with a bright-line rule: declare the real price in the rogito for transparency, but pay proportional registration tax on cadastral value when both parties are private individuals and the buyer elects prezzo-valore.

The policy trade-off is visible in every luxury market. Owners pay low tax relative to market wealth, while municipalities sometimes argue cadastral bases lag behind gentrification. Occasional finance laws adjust multipliers or launch partial revaluations, but day-to-day tax still flows from rendita tables, not from OMI market observatory quotes.

Developer sales are the major exception. When the seller is VAT-registered, IVA applies to the commercial price. A new-build loft sold by a constructor triggers 10% VAT on €850,000, not 9% on cadastral value. That is why identical-looking apartments can produce radically different tax outcomes depending on seller type. The [Italy registration tax on property](/guides/italy-registration-tax-property/) guide walks through private versus developer mechanics in full.

## How does the prezzo-valore method affect registration tax and capital gains?

Prezzo-valore lowers registration tax at purchase by replacing market price with valore catastale as the tax base, but it does not change the cost basis for capital gains when you sell within five years. Purchase tax savings and exit tax exposure must be modelled separately.

On a private resale, the notary applies 2% or 9% to valore catastale once prezzo-valore is elected in the deed. The commercial price still appears in the contract. Agenzia delle Entrate accepts the cadastral base and typically does not audit the declared price for registration tax purposes on that transaction.

At sale, plusvalenza rules look at documented acquisition cost. If you bought for €600,000 but paid registration tax on €95,000 cadastral value, your CGT calculation still starts from €600,000 plus eligible costs (notary, agency, certain improvements), not from €95,000. Prezzo-valore is a purchase-tax election, not a permanent reduction in economic cost.

| Stage | Tax | Base used | Prezzo-valore effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Private purchase (second home) | Registration tax 9% | Valore catastale | Large reduction vs market price |
| Private purchase (prima casa) | Registration tax 2% | Valore catastale | Reduction; luxury A/1, A/8, A/9 still at 9% |
| Developer purchase | VAT 4% / 10% / 22% | Commercial price | Prezzo-valore not available |
| Sale within 5 years | CGT 26% or IRPEF | Documented purchase cost + costs | No reduction from cadastral purchase tax |
| Sale after 5 years | CGT for individuals | N/A | Generally exempt on residential buildings |
| Annual ownership | IMU | Valore catastale (if due) | Same cadastral base, municipal rates |

A British investor who flips a Puglia villa within three years might save €40,000 on registration tax at entry yet owe 26% on the full capital gain at exit. Hold-period planning and residency documentation matter more than cadastral discount at purchase. ## How do you read a visura catastale before buying?

A visura catastale is the cadastral extract that lists rendita catastale, categoria, classe, surface, and map references for a unit. You should review it during due diligence, not only on the morning of the rogito.

Request the visura from Agenzia delle Entrate (online with SPID or through your geometra) or obtain a recent copy from the seller. Match the extract to the unit you are buying: wrong floor, sub-unit, or garage compartment errors surface constantly in urban buildings.

| Visura field | What it tells you | Buyer action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Rendita catastale | Annual cadastral income in euros | Apply × 1.05 × multiplier for valore catastale |
| Categoria catastale | A/2, A/8, C/6, etc. | Confirms prima casa eligibility and IMU exposure |
| Classe | Quality band within category | Explains rendita level vs similar units |
| Consistenza | Registered rooms / surface | Compare to actual layout; discrepancies need geometra |
| Identificativi catastali | Foglio, particella, subalterno | Must match land registry (conservatoria) entry |
| Zona censuaria | Census zone code | Drives rendita tables for the municipality |

Red flags include rendita stuck at historic lows after luxury renovation, categoria A/8 on a property marketed as a simple country house, or accessory units not merged with the main dwelling. Any mismatch between visura and conservatoria maps can delay mortgage approval and rogito. Professional [due diligence on Italy property](/guides/due-diligence-italy-property/) should include cadastral alignment, not only title checks.

Your notary also orders visure at closing, but early review lets you renegotiate if tax exposure or compliance work exceeds expectations. Pair cadastral review with the [notaio role in Italy property transactions](/guides/notaio-italy-property-role/) so you know which documents the notary re-verifies and which gaps remain your consultant's job.

## When does cadastral revaluation happen in Italy?

Cadastral income and multipliers change through parliamentary finance laws and property-specific surveys, not automatically when neighbourhood prices rise. Individual rendita updates when officials reclassify a unit after works, voluntary filing, or cadastral inspection.

National reforms set the architecture. The 2006 prezzo-valore law paired cadastral bases with registration tax and adjusted multipliers. Subsequent legge finanziaria packages tweak coefficients, IMU deductions, or launch targeted revaluations of certain categories. There is no annual mark-to-market of every home.

Property-level changes trigger when:

- A voluntary dichiarazione di consistenza reports new surface or layout after renovation.
- Works increase quality enough to move classe or categoria.
- Agenzia delle Entrate conducts accertamento catastale and raises rendita.
- Succession or division creates new subalterni with fresh classifications.

Buyers renovating heavily should budget for geometra filings and potential rendita increases that lift future IMU and registration tax on future sales. A low rendita today is not a permanent tax shield if you add floor area or upgrade finishes.

OMI (Osservatorio del Mercato Immobiliare) publishes market ranges used by banks and analysts, but OMI values do not replace rendita in tax formulas. Lenders may stress-test affordability against market price while tax still references cadastral arithmetic, a split that confuses foreign spreadsheets.

## How does cadastral value affect IMU and registration tax together?

IMU and registration tax both start from rendita-driven valore catastale on private transactions, but they apply at different moments and carry different exemptions. Registration tax is a one-time purchase levy; IMU is annual unless prima casa exemption applies.

IMU calculation uses valore catastale as base, then municipal rate, deductions, and abatements. Typical effective IMU ranges from roughly 0.4% to 1.06% of valore catastale per year depending on comune and whether the home is primary or secondary. Luxury categories A/1, A/8, and A/9 pay IMU even when used as abitazione principale.

| Tax | Timing | Base | Non-resident second home (illustrative) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Registration tax | At rogito | Valore catastale × 9% | €189,000 base → €17,010 tax |
| IMU | June and December instalments | Valore catastale × municipal rate | €189,000 base × 0.86% → ~€1,623/year |
| Registration (prima casa eligible) | At rogito | Valore catastale × 2% | €173,250 base → €3,465 tax |
| IMU (prima casa, non-luxury) | N/A if exempt | Exempt standard A/2–A/7 | €0 IMU if conditions met |

Because both taxes reference the same cadastral base, a unit with artificially low rendita reduces lifetime tax more than a one-time negotiation on agency commission. Conversely, an upcoming cadastral revision can raise both future IMU and the registration tax a future buyer pays if you sell privately.

Non-resident owners holding holiday homes should model IMU alongside rental yield. A €650,000 Ligurian flat with €140,000 valore catastale might carry modest IMU yet still attract 26% CGT on a profitable sale within five years. The [IMU property tax in Italy](/guides/imu-property-tax-italy/) guide details municipal variations, payment deadlines, and prima casa exemptions.

Registration tax interacts with IMU at purchase only. Choosing prezzo-valore does not cap IMU later unless rendita itself is low. Buying from a developer with VAT on full price can produce high entry tax while cadastral values remain moderate for ongoing IMU, another reason to compare seller type early.

## What cadastral checks should foreign buyers complete?

Foreign buyers should verify rendita, categoria, conservatoria consistency, and planned use before deposit. Cadastral errors are fixable but expensive when discovered after a compromesso.

Minimum checklist:

1. Obtain visura catastale dated within the last few months for every subalterno included in the sale (main dwelling, garage, cantina).
2. Recompute valore catastale with the correct multiplier for your residency plan (second home 120, prima casa 110 if eligible).
3. Confirm categoria is not A/1, A/8, or A/9 if you expect 2% registration tax and IMU exemption as prima casa.
4. Cross-check foglio/particella/subalterno against the conservatoria hypothec and ownership report.
5. Ask whether recent renovations triggered, or still require, cadastral updates that could raise rendita after closing.
6. Retain rogito and payment proofs for future CGT even if purchase tax used cadastral value.

EU and non-EU buyers follow identical cadastral rules. Residency elections affect multipliers and IMU, not access to visure. If you cannot visit, authorise your lawyer or geometra to pull extracts digitally.

Undeclared works are the silent cadastral risk. A seller marketing “three bedrooms” on a visura showing two registered rooms signals potential abusi edilizi and a future rendita jump once regularised. Factor remediation cost into price, not only cosmetic renovation.

## Summary

Cadastral value Italy property taxation rests on a simple formula, rendita catastale × 1.05 × statutory multiplier, but the implications ripple through purchase tax, annual IMU, and resale planning. Market price tells you what you pay the seller; valore catastale tells you what the state uses for most levies on private resales. Prezzo-valore converts that gap into lawful purchase-tax savings while capital gains still track real economic cost.

Treat the visura catastale as a core diligence document alongside title and mortgage reports. Confirm categoria, rendita, and map IDs early, model registration tax and IMU from cadastral bases, and keep complete rogito records for any sale within five years. Foreign buyers who master this split avoid the two classic errors: overstating entry tax on luxury resales and underestimating exit tax after a cadastral discount at purchase.

* **Scenario A: Non-resident second-home buyer on a private resale.** You pay registration tax at 9% of valore catastale, not asking price. A €800,000 Ligurian apartment with €160,000 cadastral value may trigger roughly €17,280 registration tax instead of €72,000. Model this gap before negotiating; sellers rarely discount for your tax savings unless you show the visura early.
* **Scenario B: Prima casa buyer relocating within 18 months.** Multiplier 110 and 2% registration tax apply only if categoria, residency, and size tests are met. Verify the unit is not A/1, A/8, or A/9 and that IMU exemption will apply after you register at the comune. A low rendita helps at purchase but does not override luxury-category IMU if classification is wrong.
* **Scenario C: Investor selling within five years.** Prezzo-valore reduces entry tax today but CGT at resale uses documented acquisition cost. If you bought with a large market-to-cadastral spread and plan a quick flip, keep every rogito and renovation invoice.
