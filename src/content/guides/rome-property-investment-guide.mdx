## What heritage and planning rules apply in Rome centro storico?

Rome historic center is one of Europe's densest protected urban fabrics. Buyers encounter layered controls: municipal piano regolatore (PRG), national cultural heritage code D.Lgs 42/2004, Soprintendenza Archeologia Belle Arti e Paesaggio review, and often condominio bylaws governing shared facades.

Properties marketed as "completely renovated" in Monti or near the Pantheon may still be **vincolati** (legally constrained). External color, window profiles, roof materials, and terrace enclosures can require Soprintendenza authorization before the comune issues building permits. Timelines stretch to 4-9 months on heritage interventions versus 6-10 weeks on standard internal refurbs in non-listed stock.

| Constraint type | Where it appears in Rome | Buyer impact |
|---|---|---|
| Centro storico PRG | Municipio I lanes | Volume and height limits |
| Soprintendenza vincolo | Listed palazzi, archaeological buffer | Facade and structure approvals |
| Condominio regolamento | Shared Renaissance buildings | Roof terrace and courtyard rules |
| STR density policies | Historic core micro-areas | New license scrutiny |
| Parking and ZTL | Central Rome | Limits tenant appeal, affects value |

Foreign buyers sometimes assume interior works are unrestricted. Load-bearing walls, vaulted ceilings, and fresco fragments can pull a kitchen remodel into full heritage review. Budget **25-45% premium** over standard renovation quotes when Soprintendenza oversight applies, consistent with national heritage guidance.

Before compromesso, order a certificato di vincolo and commission conformità urbanistica through a geometra. A standard agent disclosure is not a substitute for independent verification.

## How do short-term rental licensing and CIN rules work in Rome?

Italy mandates a **Codice Identificativo Nazionale (CIN)** for all short-term rentals under 30 nights, registered via the BDSR national database. Rome operators must also navigate regional CIR registration and municipal tourism rules. Non-compliance triggers fines from €800 to €8,000 and delisting from major booking platforms.

| Compliance layer | Requirement | Rome-specific note |
|---|---|---|
| National CIN | BDSR registration, display on listings | Mandatory since unified reform |
| Regional CIR | Provincial tourist accommodation code | Apply before CIN |
| SCIA / business status | Required at 3+ property threshold from 2026 | Professional operators only |
| Safety kit | Extinguishers, smoke and CO detectors | DL 145/2023 enforcement |
| Guest registration | Alloggiati Web within 24 hours | Criminal penalties if missed |
| Tassa di soggiorno | Municipal tourist tax collection | Rates vary by star classification |

Rome Municipio I has debated density caps in the densest historic lanes. Even without a Florence-style UNESCO STR ban, enforcement on unregistered units tightened through 2025. Verify whether the **specific street** already hosts saturated STR supply before assuming Jubilee demand converts to profit.

Full licensing mechanics sit in our [Italy holiday let licensing](/guides/italy-holiday-let-licensing/) guide. Purchase contingent on verified CIN transferability where an existing compliant operation is part of the thesis.

## How do IMU and prima casa rules affect Rome investors?

Tax treatment splits sharply between primary residence (prima casa) and second home or investment use (seconda casa). Most foreign Rome investors fall into seconda casa unless they relocate and register residency within 18 months.

| Tax item | Prima casa (primary residence) | Second home / investment |
|---|---|---|
| Registration tax (private sale) | 2% on cadastral value | 9% on cadastral value |
| VAT (developer sale) | 4% | 10% |
| IMU annual property tax | Exempt on primary home | ~0.76-1.06% of cadastral value |
| Rental income tax | Progressive resident rates if resident | 21% flat for non-residents |
| Residency obligation | Register within 18 months | Not applicable |

Luxury cadastral categories A/1, A/8, and A/9 (stately homes, villas, castles) are excluded from prima casa benefits even if you relocate. Many centro storico palazzi sit in these categories. Detailed comparison sits in [Italy prima casa vs second home tax](/guides/italy-prima-casa-vs-second-home-tax/) and [IMU property tax Italy](/guides/imu-property-tax-italy/).

Foreign buyers who do not plan to move to Rome should underwrite the **9% acquisition tax and ongoing IMU** from day one. Attempting prima casa treatment without genuine relocation risks clawback, 30% penalties, and interest on the tax difference.

## What due diligence matters on abusi edilizi and title in Rome?

Rome's older building stock carries elevated risk of **abusi edilizi**, unauthorized construction or plan deviations accumulated over decades. Terrace enclosures, mezzanine inserts, and changed room counts often lack matching catasto entries. Buyers inherit remediation liability after closing.

| Due diligence document | What it reveals | Risk if skipped |
|---|---|---|
| Visura catastale | Registered floor area and category | Size mismatch with physical unit |
| Conformità urbanistica | Planning compliance status | Cannot legally let or renovate |
| Condominio clearance | Outstanding spese and litigation | Hidden debt assumption |
| Mortgage / lien search | Encumbrances on title | Seller debt transfers |
| Abusi edilizi survey | Unauthorized works identification | Fines, demolition orders |
| Energy certificate (APE) | Legal requirement at sale | Cannot advertise or contract |
| CIN / STR registry check | Existing tourist license status | Business plan invalid |

Essential professional team: independent avvocato (never seller-recommended), geometra or architetto for technical survey, and a commercialista if renting. Our [due diligence Italy property](/guides/due-diligence-italy-property/) guide walks through the sequence from offer to rogito.

In condominio buildings, also request recent minutes. Rome assemblies increasingly dispute STR owners over noise and insurance. A unit legal on paper can face practical operating blocks from neighbor votes.

## Trastevere, Prati, and Ostiense: three districts compared in depth

### Trastevere: lifestyle brand with STR economics

Trastevere remains Rome's strongest foreign micro-brand: cobblestone lanes, nightlife, and walkable centro access. Two-bedroom renovated units commonly list €380k-650k. STR operators benefit from year-round city-break demand, but condominium pushback and street-level noise complaints are rising. Heritage constraints apply widely. Best for buyers accepting **3.0-4.5% gross** in exchange for resale narrative.

### Prati: residential order near Vatican institutions

Prati's grid layout, wider sidewalks, and professional tenant base suit **long-term furnished lets** to embassy staff, consultants, and medical tourists near private hospitals. Pricing runs slightly below peak Trastevere on a per-metre basis but peaks on high-floor renovated units with balconies. IMU and spese condominali run predictable. Best for lower-volatility residential income.

### Ostiense: gentrification and EUR employment spillover

Ostiense transformed from industrial fringe to restaurant and creative hub. Metro links to EUR and Termini support **younger tenant demand**. Entry pricing often €2,800-4,200/m² offers the best yield potential inside central Rome ring. Risk sits in building quality variance and slower foreign resale recognition versus Trastevere. Best for value-add investors who track urban renewal indicators.

## Red flags our researchers see repeatedly in Rome files

1. **Catasto sqm below physical area** without a documented sanatoria process.
2. **Terrace or attic conversion** marketed as bedroom without permesso.
3. **Missing CIN** on STR listings marketed as "fully licensed business."
4. **Prima casa tax quoted** to non-resident buyers who cannot qualify.
