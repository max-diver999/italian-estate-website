## Can UAE nationals buy property in Italy legally?

Yes. UAE nationals purchase Italian residential property as non-resident third-country buyers with active full reciprocity. Italy grants freehold ownership when the UAE grants equivalent rights to Italian citizens, and 2026 MAECI tables confirm full reciprocity for standard residential stock.

The distinction that trips Gulf buyers is residency versus ownership. You may acquire a Milan penthouse or Como villa without Italian residency, yet property title does not grant visa rights beyond standard Schengen tourist limits for UAE passport holders. Property law and immigration policy are separate tracks.

Your notaio checks MAECI reciprocity tables during due diligence and runs enhanced anti-money-laundering source-of-funds verification on large cross-border wires from Dubai and Abu Dhabi banks. UAE status is listed with full freehold rights for conventional urban apartments and villas outside restricted agricultural zones.

## What does full reciprocity mean for UAE buyers?

Reciprocity means Italy grants you property rights only if the UAE grants equivalent rights to Italian citizens. Article 16 of the Italian Civil Code governs non-EU acquisitions. The notaio must confirm your nationality qualifies before signing the rogito.

For UAE buyers in 2026 the answer is straightforward: full reciprocity remains active. Italian citizens may buy freehold property in designated UAE zones subject to Emirati property law. UAE citizens may acquire piena proprietà in Italy for standard residential stock. The notaio documents this check in the closing file.

Reciprocity verification is not optional paperwork. If the check fails at rogito stage, the deed is legally null. Deposits paid under compromesso may be forfeited or disputed. Verify reciprocity before transferring large AED-to-euro tranches, especially on tight completion timelines tied to European summer holidays.

## How do UAE buyers obtain a codice fiscale?

Every UAE property buyer needs an Italian codice fiscale, a sixteen-character tax identification number. Banks, the notaio, utility companies, and the Agenzia delle Entrate all require it. You cannot sign compromesso or register rogito without one.

UAE residents typically apply through the Italian embassy in Abu Dhabi or the consulate general in Dubai. Processing requires passport copy, proof of address, and completed AA4/8 form. Timelines range from one week by appointment to four weeks by post. The code itself is free.

Buyers who tour Milan or Como in person may visit Agenzia delle Entrate offices in Italy for immediate issuance. Remote buyers often grant power of attorney to an Italian lawyer to secure the code before returning to Dubai.

Insider tip from our UAE buyer files: apply for codice fiscale before you make a binding offer, not after. Dubai consulate appointment slots fill before Ramadan and European summer viewing peaks. A missing tax ID blocks notary file opening and delays anti-money-laundering clearance on escrow wires.

## What IMU and rental tax rules apply to UAE owners?

Non-resident UAE owners pay annual IMU municipal tax on secondary homes in Italy. Rates vary by comune and property class, typically 0.4-1.06% of cadastral value multiplied by municipal coefficients. IMU applies whether you personal-use the asset or leave it empty.

Short-term rental income faces 26% cedolare secca for non-resident landlords using tourist lettings. Long-term leases may use alternative flat or progressive regimes depending on election at filing. All STR activity requires a national CIN and local SCIA compliance where applicable.

| Tax topic | Italy (typical non-resident) | UAE consideration |
|---|---|---|
| Tourist rental income (STR) | 26% cedolare secca | Report per UAE personal tax status |
| Long-term rental | Flat or progressive options | Same cross-border review |
| Annual property tax | IMU on secondary homes | Budget in euro, not AED |
| Purchase closing | 9% registration on second home | Wire compliance on full tranche |
| Capital gain on sale | Italian rules may apply | Home-country reporting may apply |

UAE personal income tax treatment depends on individual residency status and requires specialist advice. Italy-UAE double taxation arrangements do not eliminate compliance work on rental reporting.

The Elective Residence Visa (Residenza Elettiva) targets non-EU nationals with passive income who will not work in Italy. Official minimum passive income is €31,160 per year for a single applicant. Dubai consulate practice often expects higher documented passive streams before approval.

You do not have to buy property to obtain ERV. A registered twelve-month lease can satisfy address requirements. Purchasing a high-quality Milan or Como home can strengthen the file by demonstrating long-term commitment and stable housing costs.

ERV prohibits employment and self-employment in Italy in most consular interpretations. Active Dubai earners may prefer the Investor Visa route, which requires qualifying capital deployment including eligible combinations of government bonds, company investment, and in some structures property-linked thresholds.

Property purchase alone does not satisfy Investor Visa capital rules without the full qualifying portfolio.
