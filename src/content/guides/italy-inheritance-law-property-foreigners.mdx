## What Is Legittima and Why Does It Affect Foreign Property Owners?

Legittima is Italy's mandatory forced heirship regime codified in Articles 536 through 548 of the Italian Civil Code. It reserves a non-negotiable portion of every estate for specific close relatives, regardless of what a will says or where the deceased was tax-resident.

For foreign buyers who treat Italian property as a holiday home or investment asset, legittima creates a direct conflict with Anglo-American estate planning habits. A US or UK will that leaves everything to one child, or to a surviving partner alone, may be partially void in Italy if protected heirs exist. The Italian portion of the estate must still satisfy reserved quotas.

The freely disposable quota (quota disponibile) is whatever remains after legittima shares are calculated. Only that slice can be redirected by will to non-protected beneficiaries, charities, or friends. Attempting to give away more triggers a reduction action (azione di riduzione) that clawed-back gifts and will provisions can unwind.

This matters at two moments: when you write a will covering Italian assets, and when heirs inherit. A property bought in Milan, Tuscany, or Puglia sits under Italian jurisdiction even if the owner lived in London, New York, or Sydney for decades.

Reserved quotas depend on which protected heirs survive the deceased. The table below reflects the standard legittima calculations under the Civil Code as applied in 2026.

| Family situation | Spouse's reserved share | Children's reserved share | Parents' reserved share | Freely disposable quota |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Spouse only (no children, no parents) | 50% |, |, | 50% |
| One child, no spouse |, | 50% |, | 50% |
| Spouse + one child | 25% | 50% |, | 25% |
| Spouse + two or more children | 25% | 50% (divided equally) |, | 25% |
| Two or more children, no spouse |, | 66.67% (divided equally) |, | 33.33% |
| Spouse + parents, no children | 50% |, | 33.33% | 16.67% |
| Parents only, no spouse or children |, |, | 33.33% | 66.67% |

Several practical points follow from this structure. Children always receive the largest protected block when present. A surviving spouse never takes the entire estate if children exist. Parents enter the calculation only when there is no surviving spouse and no children.

Lifetime gifts (donazioni) made within certain periods before death count toward legittima calculations. Heirs who received less than their reserved share can demand equalization from the estate or from other beneficiaries. This is why pre-death transfers of Italian property to one child, without adjusting for legittima, frequently trigger litigation.

Foreign owners who want a specific child to receive a holiday villa outright should model legittima with an Italian avvocato before signing any deed, not after the owner's death.

Regulation (EU) No 650/2012, in force since 17 August 2015, harmonizes cross-border succession for EU nationals. It does not replace Italian forced heirship for everyone, but it gives qualifying owners a meaningful choice.

The default rule: succession law of the country where the deceased was habitually resident at death governs the estate as a whole. For a British national living permanently in Rome, Italian law likely applies to worldwide assets unless an election is made.

The election rule: any person may choose the law of their nationality to govern succession. The choice must be express, in a will or separate declaration meeting formal requirements. A British citizen who remains UK-tax-resident but owns a Tuscan farmhouse can elect English law, subject to how Italian courts interpret the interaction with immovable property rules.

Italian real estate always remains subject to Italian land registry, cadastral, and tax formalities regardless of which succession law applies. Choosing foreign law does not remove the obligation to file a dichiarazione di successione or pay imposta di successione on Italian-sited assets.

| Owner profile | Default governing law | Can elect nationality law? | Italian formalities still required? |
|---|---|---|---|
| EU national, resident in Italy | Italian law | Yes | Yes, registration, tax, legittima review |
| EU national, resident abroad | Law of last habitual residence | Yes | Yes for Italian property |
| Non-EU national (US, UK post-Brexit, Australia) | Typically Italian law for Italian assets | Limited, depends on treaties and will form | Yes |
| Dual EU nationality holder | Either nationality may be elected | Yes, must specify clearly | Yes |

Non-EU owners lack the automatic EU election mechanism but may still achieve partial planning through international wills, matrimonial property regimes, and lifetime usufruct structures. Coordination between an Italian succession specialist and a home-country estate lawyer is not optional for these profiles.

## What Succession Tax Rates Apply to Italian Property in 2026?

Italian holding costs include IMU on cadastral value, condominium spese, insurance, and flat rental tax under cedolare secca or ordinary IRPEF regimes. Second-home registration tax at 9% on cadastral lines often exceeds notary fees on mid-market tickets — commercialista should confirm election before compromesso deposit authorization.

Imposta di successione and imposta di donazione share the same rate brackets. Tax is due on the net value inherited or received as a gift, after deductions for documented debts and expenses.

| Relationship to deceased | Tax rate | Exemption per beneficiary | Example: €800,000 apartment inherited |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spouse | 4% | €1,000,000 | €0 tax (below exemption) |
| Direct descendants (children, grandchildren) | 4% | €1,000,000 | €0 tax if sole heir under exemption |
| Direct ascendants (parents, grandparents) | 4% | €1,000,000 | €0 tax under exemption threshold |
| Siblings | 6% | €100,000 | €42,000 tax |
| Relatives up to fourth degree | 6% | €100,000 | €42,000 tax |
| Unrelated persons | 8% | €1,500,000 | €0 if under exemption; 8% above |

The taxable base for real estate is generally the cadastral value (valore catastale) or declared market value, whichever the Agenzia delle Entrate accepts. Mortgage debt secured on the property reduces the taxable base. Heirs must file Form FP1 (dichiarazione di successione) within 12 months of death through a licensed notaio or authorized professional.

Payment is typically due within 60 days of filing. Late filing triggers penalties from 120% to 240% of the tax due, plus interest. Municipalities may also assess additional local charges on property transfers in specific regions.

Flat tax residents under [Article 24-bis](/guides/italy-flat-tax-regime-new-residents/) enjoy broad exemptions on foreign assets and foreign-sourced income, but Italian property remains fully inside the succession tax net. Buying a Milan apartment while on the lump-sum regime does not shield heirs from imposta di successione.

## Which Will Format Should Foreign Owners Use for Italian Assets?

EU citizens purchase on equal terms with Italians; non-EU buyers from reciprocity countries need codice fiscale, notary-led rogito, and typically 10-15% closing costs on second homes. Non-resident mortgage LTV often caps at 50-60% through Italian banks with income verified abroad — budget equity before negotiating off-plan or renovation-heavy rural tickets.


**Holographic will (testamento olografo):** Entirely handwritten by the testator, dated, and signed. No witnesses or notaio required. Cost-effective and private, but vulnerable to challenges over handwriting authenticity. Commonly used by Italian nationals; foreign owners who cannot write in Italian risk ambiguity.

**Public will (testamento pubblico):** Drafted by a notaio, read aloud, signed by the testator and witnesses. Highest formal security under Italian law. Recommended when legittima calculations are complex or when the testator wants a professional record in Italian.

**International will (testamento internazionale):** Under the 1973 Washington Convention, executed before a notaio, authorized person, or consular official. Combines civil-law formalities with cross-border recognition. Useful for owners who maintain assets in multiple countries.

Foreign wills drawn in the owner's home country may be recognized under the 1961 Hague Convention on the Conflict of Laws Relating to the Form of Testamentary Dispositions, provided the form complies with the law of the place of execution, the testator's nationality, or the location of assets. Recognition is not automatic: an Italian court or notaio must validate the document, often requiring an apostilled copy and sworn translation.

Best practice for foreign owners: maintain one coordinated estate plan with an Italian will or Italian election clause covering real property, plus a home-country will covering other assets. The two documents must reference each other to avoid partial revocation conflicts.

The usufructuary (usufruttuario) enjoys living in the property, collecting rent, and controlling day-to-day use. The bare owner (nudo proprietario) holds title but cannot use the asset until usufruct ends, typically on death of the usufruct holder.

Tax authorities value nuda proprietà at a discounted percentage based on the usufructuary's age at transfer, per tables published by the Agenzia delle Entrate. A 70-year-old parent retaining usufruct while transferring nuda proprietà to a child can reduce the child's immediate gift tax base significantly compared with a full ownership transfer.

On the parent's death, the usufruct extinguishes and the child consolidates full ownership, often with a lower incremental succession tax than if full title had passed at death.

| Structure | Lifetime transfer tax | Tax on parent's death | Practical use case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full ownership gift to child | Higher base, full market value | May trigger additional succession adjustments | Rarely optimal with legittima |
| Usufruct retained + nuda proprietà to child | Discounted base on bare ownership share | Usufruct ends; child may consolidate with limited extra tax | Parent retains use of holiday home |
| Lifetime sale at market price | Registration tax + capital gains if applicable | Clean base for heir at purchase price | Third-party sale, not family planning |

These structures must respect legittima. Discounting ownership to one child still requires equalization among protected heirs. Any plan that uses usufruct/nuda proprietà without a full legittima model is a litigation template.

**Months 0-1 (immediate):** Obtain death certificate, locate will, secure property (change locks, notify condominium administrator, pause utilities if vacant). Appoint an Italian avvocato or notaio to open the succession file.

**Months 1-3:** Compile heir documentation (birth and marriage certificates, certificates of family status, foreign documents apostilled and translated). Submit will for publication (pubblicazione di testamento) if applicable. Obtain cadastral extracts and property valuations.

**Months 3-6:** Prepare and file dichiarazione di successione. Pay imposta di successione. If heirs disagree on shares, negotiation or mediation occurs here; contested files stall.

**Months 6-12:** Register heir transfer in land registry (voltura catastale) through notaio. Update utility contracts and condominium records. If property is rented, notify tenants and adjust lease administration.

**Months 12-36 (contested or cross-border):** Court actions on legittima reductions, foreign will validation, or multi-jurisdiction tax disputes extend the timeline. Italian courts in major cities face backlogs of 18 to 24 months for civil cases.

Executors and heirs abroad often grant power of attorney to an Italian representative. See [power of attorney rules for Italian property](/guides/power-of-attorney-property-italy/) for formal requirements. Remote heirs who delay filing beyond 12 months face automatic penalties.

**Step 1, Map protected heirs now.** List spouse, children, and living parents. Calculate legittima shares on the intended purchase price. If the numbers conflict with your wishes, adjust expectations or explore usufruct structures before the rogito.

**Step 2, Decide governing law and will format.** EU nationals should explicitly elect nationality law in a will if that aligns with planning goals. Non-EU nationals should assume Italian material rules apply to the property and draft an Italian-compliant clause.

**Step 3, Coordinate matrimonial property regime.** Community of property regimes in the owner's home country may interact unexpectedly with Italian succession. French, Spanish, and Italian community regimes each produce different results on death. A matrimonial property election (pattrimoniale) may be needed.

**Step 4, Document funding source.** Heirs must explain acquisition funds on succession filing. Mortgages reduce taxable base. Undocumented cash purchases create scrutiny. Maintain the same acquisition trail emphasized in [buying as a foreigner](/guides/buy-property-italy-foreigner/) guides.

Owners who later sell within five years face capital gains rules detailed in the [cost of buying guide](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-italy/). Long-term holders who pass property by inheritance trigger a different tax path that rewards early planning.

## Insider Tip: File the Dichiarazione Before Dividing Personal Belongings

Many foreign families fly to Italy after a parent's death, occupy the property, distribute furniture, and only then contact a notaio. That sequence creates two problems that Italian Estate legal partners see repeatedly in 2026 files.

First, taking possession and selectively removing assets can be interpreted as tacit acceptance of the inheritance (accettazione tacita), binding an heir to debts they never assessed. Second, the 12-month filing clock runs from death, not from when heirs "feel ready." Penalties apply even when no succession tax is ultimately owed.

The safer sequence: appoint an Italian professional within 30 days, inventory the property without removing items, obtain a certificate of occupancy if heirs will use the home temporarily, and file the dichiarazione di successione before any voluntary distribution of contents. If an heir intends to renounce, they must do so formally and before accepting any benefit.

This is especially critical for multi-heir families where one sibling uses the holiday home and others live abroad. Usage without a recorded succession agreement breeds disputes that extend probate past 24 months and erode property value through unpaid [IMU](/guides/imu-property-tax-italy/) and condominium arrears.

## How Does Italian Inheritance Law Connect to Your Purchase Strategy?

Inheritance rules shape who ultimately holds title, not just how much tax heirs pay. A foreign owner who buys through a company, holds through a trust, or joint-titles with a spouse triggers different succession outcomes in each case. Italian law does not recognize Anglo-American living trusts the way US courts do; direct personal ownership remains the default for most [foreign buyers](/guides/buy-property-italy-foreigner/).

The [notaio](/guides/notaio-italy-property-role/) who handled your purchase deed can often recommend a succession specialist, but the notaio does not represent heirs on death. Separate counsel is required.

For owners considering relocation and tax residency, the interaction between inheritance tax and the [flat tax regime](/guides/italy-flat-tax-regime-new-residents/) should be modelled together. The regime exempts foreign wealth from Italian gift and succession tax, but Italian bricks-and-mortar never qualifies for that shield.
