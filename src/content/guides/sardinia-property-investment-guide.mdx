## Why Is Sardinia Different from Mainland Italy for Property Investors?

Sardinia is not a discounted southern Italy play. The island operates as a dual market: ultra-luxury northern coast (Costa Smeralda, Gallura, northeast Riviera) priced against the French Riviera and Balearics, and a more accessible southern and western coast where Cagliari and Alghero offer urban and coastal entry points at €2,200-4,200/m².

Foreign buyer participation in Gallura province reaches 35-42% of transactions — among the highest regional concentrations in Italy. German and Swiss families have held Sardinian coastal property for two generations. Nordic buyers expanded into Alghero and the southwest after 2022 as Mediterranean pricing repriced. This buyer depth creates resale liquidity that Sicily's frontier interior zones cannot match, even when Sicilian gross yields run 200-400 basis points higher.

The investment trade-off is explicit. Sardinia delivers 3-6% gross yields versus Sicily's 6-10% and Puglia's 5-8%. In return, investors receive established luxury branding, Olbia airport connectivity to Munich and Zurich, and a northern coast where €1M+ properties still find German-speaking buyers within 6-9 months. Choose Sardinia when capital preservation and coastal lifestyle income matter more than yield maximisation.

## What Makes Costa Smeralda the Ultra-Luxury Anchor?

Costa Smeralda is a branded luxury micro-market, not a municipal boundary. Developed from 1962 by the Aga Khan consortium, the 55-kilometre coastline between Olbia and Arzachena commands pricing that ignores Sardinia regional averages entirely. Sea-view villas in Porto Cervo, Romazzino, and Poltu Quatu trade at €8,000-12,000/m². Trophy assets with private moorings and architect-designed estates exceed €15,000/m² on discrete transactions.

Investment logic here is wealth preservation, not yield hunting. A €2.5M Porto Cervo villa generating €90,000-€110,000 gross STR annually delivers 3.6-4.4% gross before management, IMU, and concession fees. Capital appreciation in prime Costa Smeralda has tracked 2-3.5% annually over the past decade according to Nomisma luxury coastal indices — resilient through European downturns because buyer demand is driven by UHNW diversification, not domestic mortgage credit.

Foreign buyer profile in Costa Smeralda skews German (32%), Swiss (18%), UK (12%), and Gulf/Nordic (combined 15%). Transactions are often cash or low-LTV financing. Due diligence priorities differ from mainland Italy: maritime concessions on private beaches and jetties, landscape restrictions under Gallura planning rules, and building permits for any renovation visible from the coastline.

## How Do Olbia, Alghero, and Cagliari Compare as Investment Cities?

### Olbia: Gateway to Gallura and STR Operations Hub

Olbia is Sardinia's tourism infrastructure capital. Olbia-Costa Smeralda airport processed 3.2M passengers in 2025 with direct flights from Munich, Zurich, Geneva, Stockholm, and London. The city itself trades at €2,800-4,200/m² for centro apartments and €3,500-5,500/m² for coastal units toward Pittulongu and Porto Istana.

Investment case: Olbia captures Costa Smeralda spillover demand at 30-40% discount to Porto Cervo pricing. STR operators achieve 5-6% gross yields on well-managed two-bedroom apartments with marina or beach proximity. Long-term rental demand from airport and port workers provides off-season income floor. Foreign buyer share in Olbia province exceeds 35%.

Neighbourhood positioning:
- Centro storico: €2,800-3,600/m², renovation opportunities, walkable dining
- Pittulongu beach strip: €3,800-5,500/m², premium STR, marina access
- Portside and industrial fringe: €2,200-3,000/m², long-term rental focus, 5-5.5% yields
- Countryside toward Arzachena: €2,500-4,000/m², villa STR with pool premium

Red flags: Properties marketed with "Costa Smeralda proximity" may sit 25+ kilometres from Porto Cervo. Verify actual drive time and STR licensing per comune. Condominium blocks increasingly restrict tourist rentals — obtain written regolamento approval before offer.

### Alghero: Northwest Value and Nordic Buyer Growth

Alghero combines Catalan heritage architecture, Fertilia airport connectivity, and northwest coast beaches at prices 15-25% below comparable Olbia listings. Centro storico apartments trade €2,500-3,800/m². Coastal zones toward Maria Pia and Le Bombarde reach €3,200-4,200/m². Foreign buyer growth from Sweden, Norway, and Denmark accelerated post-2022 as buyers priced out French Atlantic coast and Menorca.

Investment case: Alghero delivers the strongest yield-value balance on Sardinia's coast. Two-bedroom STR apartments generate €28,000-€38,000 gross annually with 65-75% summer occupancy at €140-€200 nightly. Gross yields of 5-6% are achievable on €350,000-€450,000 purchases — the highest coastal returns on the island outside interior zones.

Buyer profile: Nordic families (28%), German second-home owners (22%), Italian mainland investors (25%), UK post-Brexit buyers (12%). Median foreign transaction: €320,000-€480,000.

Red flags: Historic centre properties carry Soprintendenza restrictions. Coastal environmental overlays affect ground-floor extensions. Airport noise corridor affects Fertilia zone pricing — verify flight path maps before purchase.

### Cagliari: Urban Stability and Year-Round Rental Demand

Cagliari is Sardinia's administrative and university capital with 155,000 city residents and a metro population exceeding 430,000. The city offers something northern Sardinia cannot: year-round tenant demand independent of summer tourism.

Investment case: Castello and Marina districts trade €2,800-3,800/m² for renovated units. Poetto beachfront reaches €3,500-5,000/m². Long-term furnished rentals to university staff, hospital workers, and civil servants generate €900-€1,400 monthly on €250,000-€350,000 purchases — 4-5.5% gross yields with lower vacancy than STR-dependent northern coast.

Foreign buyer share remains lower than Gallura (15-18%) but is rising as remote workers target Mediterranean lifestyle with urban infrastructure. Cagliari-Elmas airport expansion and PNRR-funded tram extensions support medium-term appreciation of 2-3% annually in central districts.

Neighbourhood map:
- Castello / Marina: €3,000-3,800/m², heritage premium, tourist footfall
- Stampace / Villanova: €2,400-3,200/m², authentic residential, student demand
- Poetto lungomare: €3,500-5,000/m², beach STR blend, seasonal premium
- Pirri / Monserrato periphery: €1,800-2,400/m², yield focus, 5-5.5% gross

Red flags: Centro storico parking scarcity limits STR appeal for car-dependent guests. Some peripheral social housing zones should be avoided — verify comune socio-economic maps with local agent.

### Summer STR Peak Dynamics

Northern Sardinia STR revenue concentrates sharply. July and August generate 55-65% of annual gross STR income on typical coastal apartments. June and September contribute 20-25%. April-May and October shoulder seasons are growing but still represent under 15% combined in most Gallura listings.

Implications for investors:
- Maintain 4-6 months operating reserve for off-season carrying costs
- Price weekly rates aggressively in July-August (€1,800-€4,500/week for premium two-bedroom)
- Invest in professional photography and dynamic pricing — unmanaged listings lose 20-30% vs professionally operated comparables
- Consider hybrid strategy: 6-month STR contract in summer, 6-month long-term autumn-spring at reduced rate

Insider tip from our Sardinia transaction files: sellers in Olbia and Pittulongu routinely market apartments using gross STR figures calculated on 12 weeks at peak August nightly rates (€250-€350/night) while ignoring May and October discount periods and 15-20% platform fees. Underwrite on 16-18 effective rental weeks at blended €155-€185 nightly, or you will overpay 10-15% relative to income-supported value. The same inflation appears in Baia Sardinia listings marketed as "Costa Smeralda" — verify comune boundaries on cadastral maps, not agency brochures.

## What Are Maritime Concessions and Why Do Coastal Buyers Must Verify Them?

Maritime concessions (concessioni demaniali marittime) grant temporary use rights over state-owned maritime and lake territory managed by Demanio dello Stato and regional demanio authorities. In Sardinia, concessions affect:

- Private beach access in front of coastal villas
- Boat moorings, jetties, and floating docks
- Sea-level terraces, pools, and structures within the maritime band
- Beach clubs and lidos attached to residential developments
- Pathways from villas to shoreline across demanio land

Concessions are not property rights. They are licenses with fixed terms (typically 6-40 years depending on category), annual fees tied to concession type and surface area, and renewal subject to administrative discretion. A villa priced on "private beach" access may lose 20-40% value if concession renewal is denied or fees increase substantially.

Due diligence checklist for coastal Sardinia:
1. Request concession title (titolo concessorio) and verify expiry date
2. Confirm annual fee (canone) and payment status — arrears block transfer
3. Check whether concession transfers automatically to new owner or requires fresh application
4. Verify physical structures match authorised concession plan — abusivismo in demanio zone triggers demolition orders
5. Review landscape restrictions (vincolo paesaggistico) overlapping concession area
6. For STR operations, confirm concession category permits tourist use

Budget €2,000-€5,000 for specialised maritime legal review on any coastal purchase above €500,000. This is separate from standard notary due diligence covered in our [due diligence Italy property](/guides/due-diligence-italy-property/) guide.

## What Due Diligence Steps Matter Before Buying in Sardinia?

Sardinia transactions require mainland Italy due diligence plus island-specific coastal verification.

Standard checks (all zones):
- Cadastral extract (visura catastale) matching physical property
- Mortgage and lien search (visura ipotecaria)
- Condominium debt certificate (certificato liberatoria spese)
- Energy certificate (APE) — Class G properties face rental restrictions
- Planning compliance (conformità urbanistica)

Coastal-specific checks (Gallura, Alghero coast, Cagliari Poetto):
- Maritime concession title, expiry, and fee status
- Landscape restriction overlay (vincolo paesaggistico)
- Environmental impact assessments for any renovation
- STR licensing history and CIN transferability
- Condominium regolamento tourist rental clause

Purchase timeline:
- Urban Cagliari apartment: 120-150 days
- Olbia coastal with clean concession: 150-180 days
- Costa Smeralda villa with concession + landscape review: 180-240 days
- Renovation project with Soprintendenza: 12-24 months additional

Closing cost budget: 10-14% of purchase price. Full breakdown in [cost of buying property in Italy](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-italy/) and [buy property Italy as foreigner](/guides/buy-property-italy-foreigner/).

## What Are the Tax and Holding Costs for Sardinia Property?

Non-resident foreign owners face standard Italian tax framework with no Sardinia-specific exemptions.

| Cost Type | Rate/Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|

Example: €480,000 Olbia apartment, €52,800 closing, €2,400 annual IMU/TARI, €1,200 concession fee (if applicable), €26,400 gross STR → net operating income approximately €12,800 after tax and costs.

Step 1: Define priority — capital preservation (Costa Smeralda), coastal STR yield (Olbia/Alghero), or urban stability (Cagliari). Zone selection follows priority, not the reverse.

Step 2: Assemble Sardinia-experienced team — Italian property lawyer with maritime concession experience, commercialista for ownership structure, local agent with foreign buyer track record. Budget €5,000-€8,000 pre-purchase advisory.

Step 3: For any coastal property, commission maritime concession review before compromesso. Non-negotiable.

Step 4: Model STR on 16-18 effective weeks, not peak-August marketing. Use our [Italy rental yield guide](/guides/italy-rental-yield-guide/) for net calculations.

Step 5: Verify CIN, SCIA, and condominium regolamento for STR intent. Licensing rules detailed in [short-term rental rules Italy](/guides/short-term-rental-rules-italy/).
