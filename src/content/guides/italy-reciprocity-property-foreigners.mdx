## What is the Principle of Reciprocity (Art. 16)?

The Principle of Reciprocity, established under Article 16 of the Italian Civil Code, dictates that non-EU citizens can enjoy the same civil rights as Italian citizens, including property ownership, only if their home country grants equivalent rights to Italian nationals. The Italian notary must verify this condition before any property transaction can legally close.

The Italian reciprocity rule represents a fundamental legal concept in Italian private international law, designed to maintain balanced bilateral relationships between Italy and foreign nations. Under Article 16 of the Royal Decree of March 16, 1942, number 262, which governs the Italian Civil Code, the legal capacity of foreign nationals is strictly tied to the treatment of Italian citizens abroad. When a non-EU buyer intends to purchase real estate in Italy, the Italian notary, known as the notaio, holds the legal responsibility to examine the current status of the bilateral relations between Italy and the buyer's country of citizenship. If the foreign nation does not allow Italian citizens to buy property within its borders, the Italian reciprocity rule prevents the transaction from proceeding, rendering any signed deed null and void. This legal mechanism has been active for over 80 years, ensuring that property transactions comply with international treaties while protecting Italian national interests.
