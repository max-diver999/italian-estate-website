## What makes Florence different from other Italian property markets?

Florence is not a corporate gateway like Milan and not a pilgrimage spike market like Rome during Jubilee 2026. It is a UNESCO art capital where international buyers pay for irreplaceable address prestige, constrained supply inside the walls, and a permanent tourism and academic tenant base. That profile shapes every metric from gross yield compression in centro storico to resale liquidity driven by American and British lifestyle capital rather than Lombardy finance relocations.

Where the [Tuscany property investment guide](/guides/tuscany-property-investment-guide/) covers Chianti, Val d'Orcia, Maremma, and regional yield spreads, this page focuses on Florence comune only: municipal STR policy, quartiere-level pricing, student corridors, and foreign-buyer concentration on central rogiti. The [Florence area guide](/areas/florence/) remains the bottom-of-funnel district snapshot; here you get full investment underwriting for hold period, income strategy, and exit pools.

Florence recorded approximately 5,800-6,200 residential transactions annually in recent Tuscany market reports, with central quartieri absorbing disproportionate foreign enquiry. Portal aggregates in April 2026 put city asking prices at €4,737/m², up 5.43% year-on-year, outperforming Italy's national average near €2,188/m². Tuscany as a region captures 14.77% of national foreign property enquiries (Gate-away), the highest share among Italian regions, and Florence city is the default liquidity anchor within that brand.

Investors choose Florence when they accept lower gross yields than Puglia or Sicily in exchange for UNESCO scarcity, multi-generational lifestyle resale, and euro-denominated holding in one of Europe's most recognized historic cities. They avoid assuming centro storico short-term rental upside without verifying an existing license transfers with the sale.

## What are Florence property prices per square metre in 2026?

Florence property prices split sharply between UNESCO centro storico trophies, Oltrarno artisan corridors, university-adjacent value zones, and northern regeneration pockets. City-wide averages understate the spread: a Novoli two-bedroom and a Duomo-facing piano nobile both count toward the same index but behave as different asset classes with different regulation and buyer pools.

Centro storico within the UNESCO buffer, including streets radiating from the Duomo, Piazza della Signoria, and Ponte Vecchio surrounds, trades €5,500-8,000/m² for renovated apartments. Arno-facing piano nobile floors with lift access and partial monument views exceed €8,000/m². Ground-floor units with commercial use rights command premiums for gallery or retail conversion but face Soprintendenza oversight on signage and facade changes. Entry on a 75 m² two-bedroom often implies €412,500-600,000 before notary and agency costs.

Centro-adjacent fringe between Santa Maria Novella station and the historic walls offers €4,200-5,800/m². Buyers trade 5-10 minute walks to the Duomo for slightly lower tickets and occasional STR paths outside the densest ban zone, but verify comune maps street by street because UNESCO boundaries do not follow tourist mental maps.

Oltrarno, San Frediano, and Santo Spirito across the Arno ask €4,800-6,500/m² on canal-side and piazza stock. These quartieri combine walkability, artisan character, and stronger compliant short-term rental economics than centro storico. Side streets toward Porta San Frediano offer €4,200-5,200/m² with renovation upside.

Campo di Marte and Coverciano east of the centre trade €3,400-4,800/m². Proximity to Artemio Franchi stadium, Careggi hospital, and university research buildings supports long-term tenant demand from medical staff and academics. Novoli and the northern office corridor sit €3,200-4,200/m² with modern stock and lower heritage friction.

Peripheral quartieri including Isolotto and Gavinana offer €2,800-3,600/m². Yields can reach 4.5-5.2% gross on long-term leases, but resale to foreign lifestyle buyers is slower and capital appreciation lags Oltrarno by 1-2 percentage points annually on typical hold periods.

## Which Florence districts offer the best investment returns?

District selection determines whether your Florence file behaves like a compressed-yield trophy asset or a workable income property. Centro storico minimizes void risk for long-term academic tenants but caps gross yield near 2.5-3.5% on trophy tickets. Oltrarno and Campo di Marte deliver 150-300 basis points more gross income when bought with clean conformità and realistic renovation budgets.

Oltrarno suits investors who want walkable Renaissance character without centro STR prohibition. Furnished long-term rents on an 85 m² two-bedroom often run €1,400-1,900 monthly depending on floor and lift. Compliant short-term operations in permitted streets can achieve €120-185 nightly averages April-October when CIN, SCIA, and condominium authorization align. Void rates on professional long-term leases in Oltrarno typically stay under 5% annually because demand mixes locals, creatives, and visiting faculty.

San Frediano and Santo Spirito side streets trade slightly below main Oltrarno arteries but attract the same tenant profile. Renovation on 14th-17th century stock requires Soprintendenza clearance on external changes; budget €1,500-2,500/m² all-in on heavy restoration versus €800-1,200/m² on 1960s-1980s periphery blocks.

Campo di Marte suits investors targeting hospital staff, university researchers, and sports-event short stays without centro price tags. Long-term gross yields of 3.8-4.5% are achievable when purchase prices stay below €4,000/m² and units include parking or outdoor space valued by family tenants.

Novoli offers modern condominiums with lower maintenance surprise than palazzo floors. Corporate tenants from the northern business district and tram-linked commuters support 3.2-4.2% gross on unfurnished contracts. STR is possible but competes with hotel supply near the congress centre; underwrite conservatively.

Centro storico remains a prestige and owner-use asset class for most new foreign capital. Existing STR licenses grandfather with the property, creating a two-tier market: licensed units trade at yield-premium multiples, while non-licensed units price on long-term rent and lifestyle exit only. Do not extrapolate Navigli-style STR scaling from Milan into Florence centro without license verification.

## What rental yields can Florence investors realistically expect?

Gross yield on portal listings excludes IMU, cedolare secca at 21% or 26%, condominium spese, and realistic vacancy. Model net cash flow with a commercialista before comparing tickets across quartieri. Furnished twelve-month contracts and licensed STR paths carry different tax bands that can shift net returns by 150-200 basis points on identical purchase prices.

Florence gross yields look modest beside Puglia's 5-8% bands, but net stability on long-term academic and professional tenants compares favourably once you account for STR shutdown risk in banned zones and Soprintendenza delays that hit rural Tuscany harder on exit timing.

Long-term residential leases form the base case. A €480,000 Oltrarno two-bedroom renting at €1,750 monthly generates €21,000 annual gross, equal to 4.4% on purchase price. After IMU near €2,400 annually on typical cadastral values, condominium spese of €200-350 monthly, and one-month agency fee every three years, net yield before 21% cedolare often lands near 2.8-3.4%.

Centro storico at €650,000 on 80 m² with €1,600 monthly long-term rent yields 3.0% gross on paper, but buyers often pay above rental-implied values because they capitalize UNESCO address prestige. Realistic gross yield on sub-3% territory is common above €7,000/m².

Licensed STR in permitted Oltrarno fringe can reach 5-6% gross on furnished stock with 65-70% peak-season occupancy, but net after 21% cedolare on a first property, platform fees, cleaning, and utilities often returns 3.5-4.2%. Shoulder months November-February thin without conference and academic conference tenants.

Student-oriented configurations near Santa Maria Novella and San Marco can achieve 4.2-4.8% gross when a 100 m² apartment is legally configured as high-quality double rooms with compliant fire exits and condominium approval. Check regolamento di condominio on subletting and maximum occupancy before purchase.

## How do UNESCO heritage rules and Florence STR caps work?

Florence combines national short-term rental law with municipal caps driven by UNESCO overcrowding concerns and housing pressure on resident populations. The result is a two-speed market: grandfathered tourist licenses inside the historic core versus long-term-only economics for most new centro purchases.

Italy requires a Codice Identificativo Nazionale (CIN) for all stays under 30 days, registered in the BDSR national database and displayed at the property. Non-compliance triggers fines from €800 to €8,000 and delisting from booking platforms. Florence adds municipal scrutiny on affitti brevi density in centro storico, where new tourist registrations in the UNESCO-protected buffer are rejected for standard residential conversions.

Existing licensed short-term units continue operating when owners renew annually and respect occupancy caps, typically eight guests maximum per apartment in many configurations. Buyers purchasing a grandfathered license must confirm transferability in writing with the comune and review condominium regolamento because neighbour objections can block renewals even when municipal policy allows the license.

Outside the dense ban zone, Oltrarno, Novoli, Campo di Marte, and peripheral streets still permit compliant affitti brevi when SCIA or equivalent municipal paths align and the condominium authorizes tourist use. Always request written condominium clearance before compromesso on any STR-intended purchase.

Tax treatment follows national cedolare secca: 21% flat on net rental income for your first short-term property in Italy, 26% on a second property. Managing three or more properties from 2026 triggers mandatory Partita IVA registration with VAT obligations. Platform withholding at 21% does not eliminate liability if your effective rate is 26%.

Operational requirements include Alloggiati Web guest registration within 24 hours of check-in, tourist tax collection remitted to Florence comune, fire safety equipment, and energy performance certificates displayed. Professional managers charge 20-28% of gross booking revenue in Florence because cleaning labour reflects central Italy wage levels and heritage access logistics.

Florence comune tourist tax rates and registration portals update periodically; verify current schedules with your commercialista at purchase. ## How does the Florence student rental market support investors?

The University of Florence, European University Institute proximity, and specialist academies enrol roughly 60,000 students across the metro, creating a year-round furnished rental pool distinct from pure tourism seasonality. Postgraduate researchers, Erasmus exchanges, and fashion-school placements concentrate near Santa Maria Novella, San Marco, and Campo di Marte corridors where tram and bus links reach campuses without car dependency.

Student demand favours furnished one-bedroom and two-bedroom units with reliable Wi-Fi, desk space, and washing machines. Contracts often run 10-11 months aligned with academic years, reducing August void compared to pure STR though September turnover requires refurbishment budgets. Gross yields on student-oriented stock bought below €4,000/m² can reach 4.2-4.8% when room configurations comply with fire codes and condominium rules permit shared occupancy.

Campo di Marte and Coverciano attract medical students and hospital interns tied to Careggi university hospital. Novoli draws law and economics postgraduates near the northern campus cluster. Centro-adjacent streets south of Santa Maria Novella suit short-walk demand but carry higher entry prices that compress yields unless units were acquired before recent appreciation.

Risks include condominium bans on transient use, noise complaints triggering STR scrutiny, and competition from purpose-built student housing (PBSA) projects entering the northern corridor. Underwrite student lets on 11-month occupancy at market rent, not peak September bidding wars alone.

Compared with Milan's 220,000+ metro student population, Florence's pool is smaller but more foreign-heavy, supporting English-language leases and international guarantor structures common among American and British buyers letting to US study-abroad cohorts.

## What are the costs of buying and holding Florence property?

Transaction costs on a Florence second home for a non-resident typically total 10-12% of the declared purchase price. Registration tax runs 9% on non-luxury second homes calculated on cadastral value unless you elect price-based assessment. Notary fees, agency commission of 3-4% plus VAT, and independent legal review add further layers.

Non-resident mortgage buyers should budget 50-60% LTV from Italian banks, meaning €300,000 equity on a €500,000 Oltrarno purchase before costs. Bank appraisal below agreed price reduces lendable amount. Allow three to five months from offer to rogito with financing.

IMU annual property tax on Florence investment units typically applies at 0.5-0.76% of cadastral value depending on luxury thresholds. IMU is not deductible under cedolare secca but is deductible under ordinary IRPEF rental reporting.

Condominium spese on centro palazzo floors with lift and heating often run €250-500 monthly for an 80 m² unit. Oltrarno walk-ups without lift charge less but may require extraordinary works on roof and facade every 15-20 years under condominio law.

Renovation on UNESCO fabric runs €1,500-2,500/m² all-in when Soprintendenza approval, structural reinforcement, and MEP upgrades are required. Periphery 1970s stock renovates at €800-1,200/m². Capital gains on sale within five years trigger 26% IRPEF on nominal gain for pure investors.

3. Obtain condominium clearance for intended use, especially affitti brevi in Oltrarno palazzi.
4. Review Soprintendenza constraints on windows, terraces, and facade colour before renovation budgeting.
5. Check SIMR flood maps for Arno-adjacent ground and semi-basement units.
6. Model IMU on cadastral value, not asking price.
7. Verify university-distance claims on student-rental listings against actual tram times.

Insider tip: Florence agents often market centro apartments with expected STR income based on pre-ban comparables. Underwrite on long-term rent only unless a valid transferable license is attached to the compromesso as a condition precedent.

Engage a bilingual property lawyer independent of the selling agent. Agency commission is often split with buyer-side brokers; conflicted advice is common when the same network represents both sides.
