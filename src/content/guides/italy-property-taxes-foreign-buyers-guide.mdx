# Foreign Buyers Italy Property Taxes: 2026 Hub Guide

**Quick answer:** Foreign buyers face the same Italian tax code as Italian nationals on the property itself, but most international owners land on the second-home track: 9% registration tax on resale (or 10% VAT on new builds), full annual IMU, 21% cedolare secca on typical rental income, and 26% capital gains if you sell within five years without an exemption. Home-country layers (UK SA105, US Schedule E, IVIE for Italian tax residents with foreign portfolios) stack on top. ## What property taxes do foreign buyers pay when buying in Italy?

Foreign buyers pay purchase taxes at rogito, annual ownership taxes while they hold the asset, income taxes on rent, capital gains tax on certain sales, succession tax on death transfers, and sometimes IVIE or IVAFE if they become Italian tax residents with a global balance sheet. Nationality does not change the Italian rates. Residency status and property use (primary versus second home) do.

Most international enquiries in 2026 follow the second-home path because the owner remains tax-resident in London, New York, Dubai, or Sydney while using the Italian asset for holidays and part-year letting. That path triggers 9% registration tax on private resale purchases, full IMU every June and December, and cedolare secca at 21% on the first rental unit. Buyers who relocate and register Italian residency within 18 months can access prima casa purchase rates and IMU exemption on standard residential stock.

Italian Estate models tax before yield because a gross 6% STR return in Puglia can fall to 3% net once IMU, cedolare secca, management, and UK or US home-country tax are applied. ## How does registration tax differ from VAT on Italian property?

Registration tax (imposta di registro) applies to resale purchases from private sellers. VAT (IVA) applies to new builds and developer sales within five years of construction. Foreign second-home buyers pay 9% registration tax on cadastral value for resales or 10% VAT on the agreed purchase price for new builds, plus fixed fees of 200 euros per registry line on VAT transactions.

The prezzo-valore system lets the notary calculate registration tax on cadastral value rather than market price when buying from a private individual. Cadastral value equals rendita catastale times 1.05 times 120 for second homes (110 for prima casa). On a Florence apartment with cadastral rent of 1,500 euros, the taxable base is 189,000 euros and 9% registration tax equals 17,010 euros even if the market price is 900,000 euros.

Developer sales skip percentage registration tax. The buyer pays VAT on the full price: 4% prima casa, 10% second home, 22% on luxury cadastral classes from a VAT-registered seller. Fixed imposta di registro, ipotecaria, and catastale fees of 200 euros each apply instead of percentage mortgage and land registry taxes.

| Transaction type | Seller | Second-home rate | Tax base | Fixed fees |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Resale apartment or villa | Private individual | 9% registration tax | Cadastral value (rendita x 1.05 x 120) | 50 euros mortgage + 50 euros cadastral |
| Resale (prima casa eligible) | Private individual | 2% registration tax | Cadastral value (rendita x 1.05 x 110) | 50 euros + 50 euros |
| New build under 5 years | Developer (VAT registered) | 10% VAT | Agreed purchase price | 200 euros + 200 euros + 200 euros |
| New build prima casa | Developer | 4% VAT | Agreed purchase price | 600 euros fixed total |
| Luxury new build (A/1, A/8, A/9) | Developer | 22% VAT | Agreed purchase price | 600 euros fixed total |

## What is IMU and when do non-resident owners pay it?

IMU (Imposta Municipale Unica) is Italy's annual municipal property tax. Non-resident owners pay IMU on every Italian property classified as a second home. The rate is set by each comune between 0.76% and 1.06% of the cadastral taxable base. Payments split into acconto by 16 June and saldo by 16 December via F24.

Standard primary residences are IMU-exempt, but foreign holiday-home buyers rarely qualify because exemption requires registered residenza anagrafica and habitual use as main abode. Luxury primary homes in cadastral classes A/1, A/8, and A/9 pay IMU even when used as prima casa, at a reduced municipal rate plus a 200 euro national deduction.

The IMU base uses cadastral rent revalued by 5%, multiplied by coefficient 160 for residential category A, then multiplied by the municipal aliquota. Example: cadastral rent 1,000 euros, comune rate 1.06%. Base = 1,000 x 1.05 x 160 = 168,000 euros. IMU = 168,000 x 0.0106 = 1,780.80 euros per year.

Non-resident owners typically appoint an Italian commercialista or property manager to file F24 because payment requires Italian tax credentials. IMU is not deductible against Italian cedolare secca rent but may reduce UK rental profit or US Schedule E income depending on home-country rules.

For formula detail, luxury class exceptions, and pro-rata rules when you buy mid-year, read [IMU property tax in Italy](/guides/imu-property-tax-italy/).

## Can foreigners qualify for Prima Casa tax benefits in Italy?

Foreigners can qualify for prima casa benefits if they establish legal Italian residency and register at the municipal anagrafe within 18 months of rogito. Benefits include 2% registration tax on resale (versus 9%), 4% VAT on new builds (versus 10%), and full IMU exemption on standard residential categories. Pure holiday-home buyers who remain tax-resident abroad do not qualify.

Prima casa is not a self-declaration on the purchase deed. Agenzia delle Entrate cross-checks anagrafe records. Missing the 18-month residency deadline triggers retroactive collection of the 7 percentage-point registration tax gap, a 30% penalty, and interest. Selling within five years after claiming prima casa can claw back benefits unless you buy another primary home within one year in specific replacement scenarios.

Luxury cadastral classes A/1 (stately apartments), A/8 (villas), and A/9 (castles and historic palaces) are excluded from prima casa purchase reductions and IMU exemption even when you live there full time. Many foreign buyers targeting Tuscan farmhouses or Puglia trulli fall outside luxury classes, but Milan penthouse stock often does not.

Elective residence visa holders and flat-tax new residents frequently pursue prima casa when relocation is genuine. Remote workers who keep tax residency in another country should assume second-home treatment. ## How does cedolare secca work for rental income from Italian property?

Cedolare secca is Italy's optional flat tax on rental income. Non-resident landlords elect it instead of progressive IRPEF. The standard rate is 21% on gross rent for long-term contracts and for the first short-term tourist rental property. A second property used for short-term tourist letting triggers 26% on that unit's gross rent.

The election is made when registering the lease or STR compliance file with Agenzia delle Entrate. Cedolare secca replaces Italian income tax on that rent stream. You cannot deduct IMU, maintenance, or management fees against cedolare secca, which is why high-expense properties sometimes perform better under ordinary IRPEF if your Italian marginal rate stays low. Most foreign landlords still elect cedolare secca for predictability.

Example: gross STR revenue 28,000 euros on a single Puglia trullo after platform fees are excluded from the cedolare base calculation on registered gross rent. At 21%, Italian tax is 5,880 euros. IMU of 2,000 euros and 4,000 euros management sit outside cedolare and reduce net cash either way. A second STR unit at 18,000 euros gross pays 26%, or 4,680 euros.

Concordato subsidized leases can qualify for 10% cedolare secca but require rent caps and tenant eligibility that rarely match international STR strategies. CIN registration, SCIA or SUAR municipal paths, Alloggiati Web guest reporting, and local tourist tax collection remain mandatory regardless of tax election.

## When does Italy 26% capital gains tax apply on property sales?

Italy capital gains tax (plusvalenza immobiliare) applies when an individual sells within five years of purchase or construction completion and no exemption applies. The standard route is 26% substitute tax withheld by the notary at rogito on the taxable gain. Sales after five years of ownership are generally tax-free for private residential sellers.

Taxable gain equals sale price minus documented acquisition cost (purchase price in the deed plus eligible notary and agency fees, not the lower cadastral figure used for registration tax). Inherited property is exempt regardless of holding period. Primary residence used as abitazione principale for most of the holding period is exempt even within five years if residency can be documented.

Buildable land (terreni edificabili) follows different rules and remains taxable at IRPEF rates without the 26% substitute option. Foreign sellers face the same plusvalenza rules as Italian residents. The notary acts as withholding agent when substitute tax is elected in the deed.

| Scenario | CGT status | Typical Italian rate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Second home sold after 4 years | Taxable | 26% substitute on gain |
| Second home sold after 6 years | Exempt (individual) | 0% |
| Primary residence, documented use | Exempt | 0% |
| Inherited villa sold after 2 years | Exempt | 0% |
| Buildable land any holding period | Taxable | IRPEF progressive |

UK and US sellers may owe home-country capital gains tax on the same disposal even when Italy exempts the gain after five years. Treaty foreign tax credit rules apply where both countries tax the same element. Full exemption tests and notary withholding mechanics are in [Italy capital gains tax on property](/guides/italy-capital-gains-tax-property/).

## What inheritance and succession taxes apply to foreign owners?

Foreign owners of Italian property are subject to Italian forced heirship (legittima) and succession tax (imposta di successione) on Italian-sited assets regardless of nationality. EU nationals may elect home-country succession law under Regulation 650/2012, but Italian tax on the transfer still applies to assets located in Italy.

Succession tax rates in 2026 run from 4% for spouses and direct-line heirs (with a 1,000,000 euro exemption per beneficiary) to 6% for siblings (100,000 euro exemption) to 8% for unrelated beneficiaries (1,500,000 euro exemption). Tax is calculated on the declared value at transfer, which may differ from market price.

Legittima reserves fixed shares for spouse, children, and certain ascendants. A UK or US will that leaves an Italian villa entirely to one child may be partially void if other protected heirs exist. Lifetime gifts count toward legittima calculations and can trigger reduction actions after death.

Usufruct and nuda proprietà structures split use rights from bare ownership and can reduce succession tax exposure when planned before purchase with an Italian avvocato. Probate typically completes in 6 to 12 months for cooperative estates and can extend beyond 18 months when cross-border documentation is incomplete.

Forced heirship tables, EU law election forms, and filing deadlines are in [Italy inheritance law for property foreigners](/guides/italy-inheritance-law-property-foreigners/).

## What are IVIE and IVAFE for non-residents with Italian property?

IVIE (Imposta sul valore degli immobili situati all'estero) taxes foreign real estate owned by Italian tax residents at 0.76% of foreign cadastral or assessed value. IVAFE (Imposta sul valore delle attività finanziarie detenute all'estero) taxes foreign financial assets at 0.2% (0.4% in certain low-tax jurisdictions). Pure non-residents who own only Italian property and file as non-resident in Italy do not pay IVIE on the Italian asset itself.

The distinction matters when buyers relocate under elective residence, investor visa, or the 100,000 euro flat-tax regime. Becoming Italian tax resident triggers worldwide reporting. IVIE applies to your UK or US home if you keep it while living in Italy. IVAFE applies to foreign brokerage and bank accounts above de minimis thresholds.

Italian property held while you are non-resident in Italy is subject to IMU and Italian source rent tax, not IVIE. Once you move tax residency to Italy, IMU rules on your Italian second home may change if you reclassify use, and IVIE may apply to property you still own abroad.

## What total annual tax burden should foreign owners budget?

Foreign second-home owners should budget IMU (often 1,500 to 4,000 euros on mid-market villas), TARI waste tax (200 to 800 euros), cedolare secca at 21% of gross rent if letting, local tourist tax remitted to comune on STR nights, plus home-country income tax after treaty credit. Non-letting owners still pay IMU and TARI every year.

Purchase phase adds 10% to 12% all-in on typical resale second homes before renovation. Annual carrying tax without rental income is often 2,000 to 5,000 euros on 400,000 to 700,000 euro coastal stock once IMU, TARI, and condominium spese are combined. STR operators add cedolare, management at 15% to 25% of gross, and compliance costs for CIN and municipal licensing.

| Owner profile | Purchase tax stack | Annual Italian taxes (no rent) | Annual with STR gross 30k |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Non-resident second home (resale) | 9% registro + 3% notary/agency | IMU 2k + TARI 400 | + cedolare 6.3k at 21% |
| Non-resident new build | 10% VAT + fees | IMU 2.5k + TARI 500 | + cedolare 6.3k |
| Italian resident prima casa (resale) | 2% registro | IMU 0 + TARI 350 | + cedolare if let |
| Flat-tax resident, global portfolio | 9% if second home | IMU + IVIE/IVAFE abroad | Model with commercialista |

Request three-year condominium minutes before compromesso, not just last year's ordinary budget.

## How do UK and US tax rules overlay Italian property taxes?

UK tax residents report Italian rental profit on SA105 even when Italy already taxed rent through cedolare secca. You claim Foreign Tax Credit Relief under the UK-Italy double tax treaty to avoid double taxation on the same income element. UK CGT at 18% or 24% applies on disposal gains while UK resident. Lettings relief no longer shields pure holiday homes sold after April 2020.

US citizens and green card holders report worldwide rental income on Schedule E and claim foreign tax credit on Form 1116 for Italian tax paid. Cedolare secca qualifies as creditable foreign tax in most cases. IMU is generally deductible on US Schedule E even though Italy disallows IMU against cedolare. FBAR (FinCEN 114) is required when aggregate foreign accounts exceed $10,000 at any time. FATCA Form 8938 applies at higher asset thresholds.

Neither country exempts you from Italian IMU or purchase taxes at source. The overlay is parallel filing plus treaty credit, not pick-one jurisdiction. Post-April 2025 UK reforms ended new remittance basis claims for non-domiciled residents, increasing worldwide reporting for many British buyers.

| Tax layer | Italy (non-resident) | UK overlay | US overlay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Purchase | 9% registro or 10% VAT | No direct UK purchase tax | No direct US purchase tax |
| Annual ownership | IMU + TARI | Not creditable; affects UK net rent if let | IMU deductible on Schedule E if let |
| Rental income | Cedolare 21% or 26% | SA105 + FTC | Schedule E + Form 1116 |
| Sale within 5 years | 26% plusvalenza if taxable | UK CGT + FTC | US CGT + FTC |
| Sale after 5 years | Generally exempt | UK CGT on gain | US CGT on gain |
| Death / gift | Succession tax IT | UK IHT worldwide | US estate tax rules |

## How should foreign buyers sequence tax planning before rogito?

Sequence tax planning in four phases: model purchase track (prima casa versus second home) before offer; confirm cadastral class and seller VAT status before compromesso; elect rental tax path (cedolare versus IRPEF) before first guest or tenant; document acquisition cost for future CGT and home-country base cost from day one.

Phase one at offer stage: decide whether relocation within 18 months is realistic. If not, underwrite 9% registration tax and full IMU. Phase two at compromesso: verify rendita catastale, luxury class, and whether seller is private or developer because VAT versus registration tax restructures total closing cash by five to fifteen percentage points on some tickets.

Phase three before letting: register lease or STR compliance, obtain CIN, and confirm cedolare election with commercialista. Phase four ongoing: retain rogito, F24 IMU receipts, renovation invoices in one data room for CGT basis and UK/US reporting.

Independent Italian avvocato and commercialista should review before deposit, not after. Seller-recommended advisers create conflict. Home-country CPA or chartered tax adviser should model overlay before you fix budget in euros.
