## Overview

Val d'Orcia received UNESCO World Heritage designation in 2004, protecting the iconic Tuscan hillscape that global tourism marketing uses as shorthand for Italian countryside identity. The region spans five principal comuni in Siena province: Pienza, Montalcino, San Quirico d'Orcia, Castiglione d'Orcia, and Radicofani, each with distinct per sqm bands, tourism flows, and renovation compliance profiles.

Unlike Chianti's Florence-adjacent wine tourism hub, Val d'Orcia trades car dependency for landscape authenticity. Buyers accept 90-120 minute drives to Florence Peretola airport while gaining UNESCO narrative depth, thermal spring villages like Bagno Vignoni, and Brunello DOCG branding on Montalcino corridors. H1 2025 demand ran about 25% above prior year on portal enquiry for restored casali according to market commentary citing Nomisma aggregates.
