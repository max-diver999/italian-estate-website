## Short-Term Rental Rules in Florence

Short-term rental income requires valid CIN registration, commune SCIA or SUAR paths where applicable, Alloggiati Web guest filing, and tourist tax collection remitted to comune. First-property STR income may use 21% cedolare secca; a second property triggers 26% — model net after platform fees, cleaning, and void months not peak-event screenshots alone.

The City of Florence has banned new short-term rental licenses within the UNESCO historic center. This is a hard cap, not a guideline:

- Existing licenses: Properties with STR licenses issued before the ban continue to operate legally. License holders must renew annually and comply with occupancy caps (typically max 8 guests per apartment).
- No new applications: New applications for centro storico STR are rejected. This pushes rental potential downward for new investors targeting the heart of Florence.
- Exemption: Tourist-grade hotel and bed-and-breakfast licenses (separate from residential STR) still operate and may be renewable, but these require hospitality business registration, not simple residential rental.

Secondary Districts & Oltrarno (Allowed with Registration):

- Oltrarno (across the Arno) and outer districts (Novoli, Santa Maria Novella fringe, Pignone) permit short-term rentals with proper registration.
- Requirements: Property tax registration, guest registry (libro degli ospiti), 30% income tax withholding, and municipal permission. No cap on new licenses.
- Typical yield: 4–6% gross depending on location, season, and property amenities.

Key Risk: If you purchase a centro storico property expecting STR upside, verify the license status *in writing* from the city. Many listings claim STR potential without disclosing the ban.

### Centro Storico (The Heart)

![Ponte Vecchio, Florence's iconic bridge and jewelry district](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ponte_Vecchio_Florence.jpg/1280px-Ponte_Vecchio_Florence.jpg)

The Duomo, Piazza della Signoria, Ponte Vecchio, the epicenter of tourism and Renaissance art. Prices €600k–1.5M for small-to-medium apartments (60–120 m²). Ground-floor commercial space is premium but illiquid (jewelry shops, galleries, restaurants dominate).

Yield Profile: 2–3.5% long-term only. STR banned. Best for: Prestige ownership, personal use (vacation home), or legacy investment where appreciation matters more than cash flow.

Renovation Reality: Medieval/Renaissance buildings (14th–16th century) have charm but poor insulation, narrow hallways, and flood risk. Budget €15k–25k/m² for full renovation. Plumbing and electrical work is mandatory pre-purchase.

### Oltrarno (Artisan Left Bank)

Hip, walkable, with studios, galleries, and vintage wine bars. Prices €250k–500k for spacious apartments (80–150 m²). Less tourist congestion than centro storico; more local community.

Yield Profile: 3–5% long-term; 4–6% STR (licensed). Best for: Income investors, artist/creatives, couples seeking neighborhood character.

STR Advantage: Oltrarno actively permits new STR licenses. If rental income is your priority, this district outperforms centro storico by 2–3 percentage points.

### Novoli & Northern Districts

Newly gentrified, with modern infrastructure and younger demographics. Prices €180k–350k for modern apartments (80–130 m²). Close to San Lorenzo market and tech hubs.

Yield Profile: 3–4.5% long-term; 4–5% STR. Best for: Budget-conscious entry investors, families, professionals working outside centro storico.

Trade-off: Suburban feel; less immediate tourist access, but more affordable and better for quality-of-life residents.

## Investment Risks & Considerations

Italian property risk clusters around cadastral mismatches, unauthorized layout changes, pending condominium extraordinary works, and STR licensing gaps that agents omit from English summaries. Independent avvocato and geometra review before compromesso beats post-deposit discovery of conformità blocks, CIN delisting risk, or spese spikes within the first ownership year.

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| UNESCO Restrictions | High | Research license status before purchase; Oltrarno has more upside |
| Property Age & Maintenance | High | Hire surveyor; budget 5–10% annually for repairs |
| Flood Risk | Medium | Check flood maps (SIMR); buildings on hills (Piazzale Michelangelo) are safe |
| Complexity (Italian Taxes, Codice Fiscale) | Medium | Use Italian lawyer (€2k–4k) for purchase; hire accountant (€1.5k–2.5k/year) |
| Currency (EUR) Volatility | Low–Medium | Consider EUR exposure if you're USD-based; lock in at purchase |
| Seasonal Demand Swings | Medium | Hybrid long-term + seasonal rental smooths income |

**Taxation**: As a foreign property owner, you pay:
- **Transfer tax**: 9–10% (buyer bears most)
- **Annual property tax**: 4–6% of cadastral value (often lower than market value)
- **Rental income tax**: 20–43% depending on residency status
- **Vacancy tax**: Some municipalities charge on unused properties

## Buyer Profiles & Typical Use Cases

EU citizens purchase on equal terms with Italians; non-EU buyers from reciprocity countries need codice fiscale, notary-led rogito, and typically 10-15% closing costs on second homes. Non-resident mortgage LTV often caps at 50-60% through Italian banks with income verified abroad — budget equity before negotiating off-plan or renovation-heavy rural tickets.

### 1. American Lifestyle Investors
**Profile**: Aged 45–65, discretionary income $1M+, seeking art access and part-time residence.

**Strategy**: €400k–700k property in centro-adjacent or Oltrarno; 3–4 months/year occupancy; rent out remaining months (3–4.5% yield).

**Appeal**: Piazzale Michelangelo views, Uffizi proximity, wine country weekends.

### 2. British Retirees
**Profile**: Aged 60+, seeking full-time or seasonal residence; modest rental income secondary.

**Strategy**: €250k–450k; Oltrarno or hillside (Bellosguardo) for quieter lifestyle; mostly personal use; occasional summer rentals.

**Appeal**: English-speaking community, healthcare access, walkable neighborhoods.

### 3. German / Dutch Yield Investors
**Profile**: Aged 40–55, seeking stable cash flow; treat property as diversified real-estate portfolio.

**Strategy**: €300k–500k in Oltrarno or Novoli; licensed STR or hybrid long-term + seasonal; target 4–5% gross yield; reinvest appreciation.

**Appeal**: Regulated market, property rights clarity, 5.43% YoY appreciation above EU average.
