**Quick answer:** Chianti offers restored farmhouses from €400,000 with 4-7% gross yields through agriturismo or licensed STR. Greve, Radda, Gaiole, and Castellina each differ on pricing, tourism flow, and heritage permits, read the regional [Tuscany property investment guide](/guides/tuscany-property-investment-guide/) before you commit.

## Quick Answer

Chianti offers European real estate investors a rare combination: historic vineyard properties, cultural heritage appeal, and yield potential through agriturismo or short-term rental licensing. Entry-level restored farmhouses start around €400,000; premium cases with vineyard views command €1 million-plus. Gross yields of 4–7% are achievable, with agriturismo operations delivering the most consistent returns where agricultural land use is permitted. However, heritage protections and Italian agricultural regulations require careful due diligence before purchase.

## Overview

Chianti stretches across Tuscany's central heartland, spanning parts of Siena, Florence, and Arezzo provinces. The region is globally synonymous with wine, specifically Chianti Classico and Chianti Colli Senesi designations, but it also harbors some of Italy's most charming agritourism and investment real estate.

The area encompasses four main towns: Greve in Chianti (the tourism epicentre and gateway), Radda in Chianti (medieval hill town, upscale), Gaiole in Chianti (quieter, agricultural focus), and Castellina in Chianti (wine producer stronghold). Properties in these zones range from standalone farmhouses (casali) to vineyard estates to village piazzas.

## Sub-Regions at a Glance

### Greve in Chianti
The de facto capital and wine tourism hub. Home to the famous Piazza Matteotti, hundreds of wine shops, restaurants, and a steady influx of international visitors year-round.

- Appeal: Walkable centro, short drive to wineries, established tourism infrastructure.
- Properties: Mix of apartments in historic palazzi and renovated farmhouses within 5–10 km.
- Rental potential: Strong for both long-term and short-term. Agriturismo less common due to smaller land parcels.
- Prices: Premium; €2,800–5,500/m² for centro; €2,200–3,500/m² for countryside just outside.

### Radda in Chianti
Medieval walled town with tight cobblestone centro and excellent wine credentials (Chianti Classico heartland).

- Appeal: Authenticity, upscale clientele, strong terroir reputation.
- Properties: Historic town houses and a few countryside estates within the municipality.
- Rental potential: Upscale short-term rental; selective agriturismo on larger holdings.
- Prices: €3,000–5,500/m² centro; €2,200–3,800/m² surrounding casali.

### Gaiole in Chianti
Quieter and more agricultural. Less tourism infrastructure but authentically rural and affordable by regional standards.

- Appeal: Lower prices, larger holdings, active farming culture.
- Properties: Mostly standalone casali and vineyard lots; few urban apartments.
- Rental potential: Agriturismo-focused; agricultural land use more common.
- Prices: €1,800–3,200/m² for renovated homes; land more affordable.

### Castellina in Chianti
Wine production stronghold with small historic centre and expansive wine estates surrounding.

- Appeal: Agricultural authenticity, wine credibility, lower tourism pressure than Greve.
- Properties: Vineyard parcels with residential component; estate-scale holdings.
- Rental potential: Limited short-term; strong agriturismo potential if agricultural criteria met.
- Prices: €2,000–3,800/m² for built structures.

## Yields & Rental Economics

### Agriturismo Model
Agriturismo (farm hospitality) is the highest-yielding option if your property qualifies:

- Gross yield: 5–6% annually when operating efficiently.
- Occupancy: 60–70% during shoulder and high seasons (Apr–Oct); winter 15–25%.
- Requirements:
  - Minimum 3–5 hectares of productive agricultural land.
  - Active crop, vineyard, or livestock production (not just decorative).
  - Licensed beds (typically 4–20 rooms).
  - On-premise restaurant or food service.
  - Renovation to hospitality standards (fire safety, accessibility, kitchen facilities).

- Timeline to licence: 6–12 months (permits + inspections + training).
- Running costs: Staff (8–12 full-time equivalents July–September), utilities, maintenance, insurance, licensing.

### Short-Term Rental (Affitto Breve)
Tourist-focused STR without agriturismo licensing:

- Gross yield: 3–5% annually, highly seasonal.
- Licensing: Registered with municipal authorities; fewer restrictions than agriturismo.
- Appeal: Independent owners; no restaurant obligation; lower staffing.
- Occupancy: Similar to agriturismo in tourist months; December–February very slow.

### Long-Term Residential Rental
Lower yield but stable:

- Gross yield: 2–3% annually.
- Demand: Strong from expats, remote workers.
- Tenant cycle: European standard (1-year+ agreements).

## Heritage, Agricultural, & Planning Considerations

### DOCG & Vineyard Classification
If your Chianti property includes vineyard land (or vineyard-classified soil), you inherit Chianti DOCG production rules:

- Permitted grape varieties: Sangiovese (80%+), Canaiolo, Trebbiano.
- Yield limits: production capped per hectare to maintain quality standards.
- Certification: annual declarations required; professional wine producer affiliation recommended.
- Breach consequences: fines, declassification, penalties on future sales.

Action item: Request a detailed "uso del suolo" (land-use) certificate before purchasing any Chianti property with agricultural component.

### Heritage Protection (Vincolo Paesaggistico)
Much of Chianti falls under regional landscape protection:

- Exterior alterations: Require heritage authority sign-off (Soprintendenza). Windows, doors, roof materials, colours all regulated.
- Interior work: Usually unrestricted; modern utilities, bathrooms, kitchens permitted.
- Timeline: Permit applications: 60–90 days typical.
- Cost: Professional heritage consultancy: €2,000–5,000 per project for drawings + submissions.

### Agricultural Land Conversion
To convert agricultural land (say, a small vineyard plot) to residential use:

- Procedure: Change of use application to municipal planning office + regional environmental check.
- Timeline: 4–8 months.
- Approval: Not guaranteed; Chianti municipalities are protective of agricultural identity.
- Cost: €5,000–15,000 in consultancy + permits.

## Seasonal & Market Dynamics

High season: April–October. Wine tourism peaks; rental rates at maximum.
Shoulder: March, November. Good occupancy; moderate rates.
Low season: December–February. Tourism drops 70–80%; many properties close.

Market insight: foreign buyers (mainly German, British, Dutch, North American) dominate Chianti investment. Local Tuscan buyers are less common due to prices and agricultural regulations. This diversifies your potential tenant/buyer pool but also means market trends follow international economic cycles.

## Practical Investment Workflow

1. Search & Survey (Weeks 1–4)
- Identify 3–5 candidate properties.
- Hire bilingual commercial surveyor (geometra commerciale).
- Request catasto (official property registry), planimetrie (floor plans), and DOCG/heritage status.

2. Legal Due Diligence (Weeks 5–8)
- Agricultural land-use certification (uso del suolo).
- Heritage approval status (search Soprintendenza records).
- Existing agriturismo licence, if claimed.
- Utility connections (acqua, fognatura, gas, electricity).

3. Offer & Negotiation
- Budget restoration: €1,500–3,000/m² for farmhouses (higher if heritage restrictions apply).
- Request seller disclosure on any outstanding heritage permits or liabilities.

4. Permitting & Licensing (Post-Purchase)
- File agriturismo licence application or STR registration.
- Engage heritage consultant if exterior work planned.
- Timeline: 6–12 months before revenue.

## Risks and red flags for Chianti buyers

Heritage landscape zones can block pool construction or exterior changes you assumed were standard. Agricultural land classifications may prevent full-time residential use without a change-of-use process. Always verify the seller's agriturismo or CIN documentation matches the exact cadastral parcel, not a neighbouring farmhouse. Budget 10-15% purchase costs on top of headline price, and reserve €40,000-80,000 for systems upgrades on older casali even when marketed as restored.
