Arezzo offers inland Tuscany investors the region's lowest major-city entry on Immobiliare.it April 2026 data at about €1,450 per sqm citywide, with rents at about €8.6 per sqm monthly versus regional averages near €16.4 per sqm. ## Overview

Arezzo sits in eastern Tuscany on the A1 motorway corridor between Florence and Rome, anchoring a province of hill towns, Piero della Francesca fresco heritage, and Antiques Fair weekends that concentrate STR pricing on licensed centro inventory each September. Unlike Siena's global Palio branding or Florence's Uffizi gravity, Arezzo attracts pragmatic buyers seeking Tuscany urban exposure at tickets 40-70% below Florence centro comparables.

The comune hosts about 100,000 residents with steady hospital and university employment supporting long-term lease demand independent of peak tourism weeks. Foreign enquiry remains thinner than Siena or Val d'Orcia landscape stock but domestic Italian upgraders from Florence and Rome provide resale liquidity when pricing aligns with OMI quartiere reference bands documented by independent avvocato market research rather than agent-selected comparables from stale listings.
